# Brake System

*Section code: `BR` | source PDF pages 732–771 | manual pages BR-1 to BR-40*

Covers the AW11 (Toyota MR2, 1988) hydraulic brake system: pedal/booster checks,
bleeding, master cylinder, brake booster, front and rear disc brakes (with integrated
parking-brake mechanism in the rear caliper), and brake hoses/tubes. Applies to both
**4A-GE** and **4A-GZE** (supercharged) models except where a value is called out per
engine.

> Torque is printed as **kg-cm (ft-lb, N·m)**. All three figures are transcribed exactly
> as printed; they are not converted or recomputed.

## Precautions

1. Care must be taken to replace each part properly as it could affect the performance of
   the brake system and result in a driving hazard. Replace the parts with parts of the
   same part number or equivalent.
2. It is very important to keep parts and area clean when repairing the brake system.

## Checks and Adjustments

<a id="p736"></a>
### Check and Adjustment of Brake Pedal

1. **Check that pedal height is correct, as shown.**
   Pedal height from asphalt sheet: **154 – 164 mm (6.06 – 6.46 in.)**
2. **If necessary, adjust pedal height.**
   1. If necessary, remove the instrument lower finish panel and air duct.
   2. Loosen the stop light switch lock nut.
   3. Sufficiently loosen the stop light switch.
   4. Loosen the push rod lock nut.
   5. Adjust the pedal height by turning the pedal push rod.
   6. Return the stop light switch until it lightly contacts the pedal stopper.
   7. Tighten the two lock nuts.
   8. Check that the stop lights light when the brake pedal is depressed.
   9. After adjusting the pedal height, check and adjust the pedal freeplay.
3. **Check that pedal freeplay is correct, as shown.**

   *(Single type brake booster)*
   1. Stop the engine and depress the brake pedal several times until there is no more
      vacuum left in the booster.
   2. Push in the pedal until the beginning of resistance is felt. Measure the distance,
      as shown.

   Pedal freeplay: **3 – 6 mm (0.12 – 0.24 in.)**

   > **NOTE:** The pedal freeplay is the amount of the stroke until the booster air valve
   > is moved by the pedal push rod.

   *(Tandem type brake booster)*
   1. Stop the engine and depress the brake pedal several times until there is no more
      vacuum left in the booster.
   2. Push in the pedal by hand until the beginning of the second resistance is felt.
      Measure the distance, as shown.

   Pedal freeplay: **3 – 6 mm (0.12 – 0.24 in.)**

   > **NOTE:** The freeplay to the first resistance is due to the play between the clevis
   > and pin. And it is **1 – 3 mm (0.04 – 0.12 in.)** on the pedal.
4. **If necessary, adjust pedal freeplay.**
   1. If incorrect, adjust the pedal freeplay by turning the pedal push rod.
   2. Start the engine and confirm that pedal freeplay exists.
   3. After adjusting the pedal freeplay, check the pedal height.
   4. Install the air duct and instrument lower finish panel.
5. **Check that pedal reserve distance is correct, as shown.**
   Release the parking brake. With engine running, depress the pedal and measure the pedal
   reserve distance, as shown.

   Pedal reserve distance from asphalt sheet at **50 kg (110.2 lb, 490 N)** <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: OCR read "60 kg"; page image (p.737, BR-6) clearly shows "50 kg", consistent with the printed 110.2 lb / 490 N — corrected from image -->:
   - **4A-GE:** More than **87 mm (3.43 in.)**
   - **4A-GZE:** More than **77 mm (3.03 in.)**

   If incorrect, troubleshoot the brake system.

<a id="p737"></a>
### Operational Test of Brake Booster

> **NOTE:** If available, use a brake booster tester to check the booster operating
> condition.

1. **Operating check.**
   1. Depress the brake pedal several times with the engine stopped, and check that there
      is no change in the pedal reserve distance.
   2. Depress the brake pedal and start the engine. If the pedal goes down slightly,
      operation is normal.
2. **Air tightness check.**
   1. Start the engine and stop it after one or two minutes. Depress the brake pedal
      several times slowly. If the pedal goes down furthest the first time, but gradually
      rises after the second or third time, the booster is air tight.
   2. Depress the brake pedal while the engine is running, and stop it with the pedal
      depressed. If there is no change in pedal reserve travel after holding the pedal for
      thirty seconds, the booster is air tight.

<a id="p737"></a>
### Bleeding of Brake System

> **NOTE:** If any work is done on the brake system or if air is suspected in the brake
> lines, bleed the system of air.
>
> **CAUTION:** Do not let brake fluid remain on a painted surface. Wash it off
> immediately.

1. **Fill brake reservoir with brake fluid.**
   Check the fluid level in the reservoir. If necessary, add brake fluid.
2. **Bleed master cylinder.**
   > **NOTE:** If the master cylinder was disassembled or if the reservoir becomes empty,
   > bleed the air from the master cylinder.
   1. Disconnect the brake tubes from the master cylinder.
   2. Depress the brake pedal and hold it.
   3. Block off the outlet holes with your fingers, and release the brake pedal.
   4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 three or four times.
3. **Begin bleeding air from brake cylinder with longest hydraulic line.**
   1. Connect the vinyl tube to the brake cylinder bleeder plug, and insert the other end
      of the tube in a half-full container of brake fluid.
   2. Slowly pump the brake pedal several times.
   3. While having an assistant press on the pedal, loosen the bleeder plug until fluid
      starts to run out. Then close the bleeder plug.
   4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 until there are no more air bubbles in the fluid.
   5. Tighten the bleeder plug.

   Bleeder plug tightening torque: **85 kg-cm (74 in.-lb, 8.3 N·m)**
4. **Repeat procedure for each wheel.**

<a id="p738"></a>
### Check and Adjustment of Parking Brake

1. **Check that parking brake lever travel is correct.**
   1. Pull the parking brake lever all the way up and down two or three times. Then return
      the parking brake lever.
   2. Depress the brake pedal several times.
   3. Pull the parking brake lever all the way up, and count the notches of lever travel.

   Parking brake lever travel at **20 kg (44.1 lb, 196 N): 5 – 8 clicks**

   If incorrect, adjust the parking brake.
2. **If necessary, adjust parking brake.**
   1. Pull the parking brake lever all the way up and down two or three times. Then return
      the parking brake lever.
   2. Depress the brake pedal several times.
   3. Remove the fuel tank protector.
   4. Loosen the adjusting nut and brake cable, and check that the parking brake crank
      touches the stopper pin.
   5. Stretch the brake cable by turning the adjusting nut before the parking brake crank
      begins moving.
   6. Tighten the adjusting nuts.
      > **NOTE:** Tighten the adjusting nuts so the equalizer is horizontal to the ground.

      Torque: **160 kg-cm (12 ft-lb, 16 N·m)**
   7. Install the fuel tank protector.

## Master Cylinder

<a id="p739"></a>
### Component torques

Specified torques called out on the master cylinder component diagrams (BR-8 removal,
BR-10 disassembly):

| Fastener | Torque |
| --- | --- |
| Master cylinder mounting nuts (to booster) | 130 kg-cm (9 ft-lb, 13 N·m) |
| Brake tube union nuts | 155 kg-cm (11 ft-lb, 15 N·m) |
| Reservoir union bolt | 550 kg-cm (40 ft-lb, 54 N·m) |
| Reservoir set bolt | 250 kg-cm (18 ft-lb, 25 N·m) |
| Piston stopper bolt | 100 kg-cm (7 ft-lb, 10 N·m) |

*(Figure: master cylinder exploded views — see page images p.739 (BR-8) and p.741 (BR-10).
Non-reusable parts: reservoir union gaskets, reservoir set-bolt gasket, stopper-bolt
gasket, reservoir gasket.)*

<a id="p739"></a>
### Removal of Master Cylinder

1. **Remove luggage compartment trim cover.**
   Remove the two clips after disengaging the inner part of the clip, and remove the cover.
2. **Disconnect level warning switch connector.**
3. **Take out fluid with syringe.**
   > **CAUTION:** Do not let brake fluid remain on a painted surface. Wash it off
   > immediately.
4. **Disconnect two brake tubes.**
   Using SST, disconnect two brake tubes from the master cylinder.
   SST **09751-36011**
5. **Remove master cylinder.**
   1. Remove the four nuts and check valve bracket.
   2. Remove the master cylinder from the brake booster.

<a id="p741"></a>
### Disassembly of Master Cylinder

1. **Place cylinder in vise.**
2. **Remove reservoir and hose.**
   1. Remove the set bolt from the reservoir.
   2. Remove the reservoir with the hose from the master cylinder.
3. **Remove reservoir union.**
   1. Remove the union bolt.
   2. Remove the reservoir union and two gaskets.
4. **Remove piston stopper bolt.**
   Using a screwdriver, push the pistons in all the way, and remove the piston stopper
   bolt and gasket.
5. **Remove two pistons and springs.**
   1. Push in the piston with a screwdriver and remove the snap ring with snap ring pliers.
   2. Remove the two pistons and springs from the master cylinder. If necessary, inject
      compressed air into the outlet hole to force out the piston.

<a id="p742"></a>
### Inspection of Master Cylinder

> **NOTE:** Clean the disassembled parts with compressed air.

1. **Inspect cylinder bore for rust and scoring.**
2. **Inspect cylinder for wear or damage.**
   If necessary, clean or replace the cylinder.

<a id="p743"></a>
### Assembly of Master Cylinder

1. **Apply lithium soap base glycol grease to rubber parts of piston.**
2. **Install two springs and pistons.**
   > **CAUTION:** Be careful not to damage the rubber lips on the pistons.
   1. Insert two springs and pistons straight in, not at an angle.
   2. Using snap ring pliers, install the snap ring.
3. **Install piston stopper bolt and new gasket.**
   Using a screwdriver, push the pistons in all the way and install the piston stopper bolt
   through the new gasket. Torque the bolt.
   Torque: **100 kg-cm (7 ft-lb, 10 N·m)**
4. **Install reservoir union.**
   1. Install the union through the new gaskets.
   2. Install and torque the union bolt.
   Torque: **550 kg-cm (40 ft-lb, 54 N·m)**
5. **Install reservoir and hose.**
   1. Connect the hose to union and reservoir.
   2. Install the reservoir on the master cylinder with the "MAX" mark facing toward the
      front.
   3. Install the bolt through the new washer and torque it.
   Torque: **250 kg-cm (18 ft-lb, 25 N·m)**

<a id="p745"></a>
### Installation of Master Cylinder

1. **Clean out groove on lower installation surface of master cylinder.**
2. **Adjust length of brake booster push rod before installing master cylinder.**
   (See [Installation of Brake Booster](#installation-of-brake-booster).)
3. **Install master cylinder.**
   Install the master cylinder and check valve bracket on the brake booster with four nuts.
   Torque: **130 kg-cm (9 ft-lb, 13 N·m)**
4. **Connect two brake tubes.**
   1. Finger tighten the union nuts.
   2. Using SST, torque the union nuts.
   SST **09751-36011**
   Torque: **155 kg-cm (11 ft-lb, 15 N·m)**
5. **Install luggage compartment trim cover.**
6. **Connect level warning switch connector.**
7. **Fill brake reservoir with brake fluid and bleed brake system.**
   (See [Bleeding of Brake System](#bleeding-of-brake-system).)
8. **Check for leaks.**
9. **Check and adjust brake pedal.**
   (See [Check and Adjustment of Brake Pedal](#check-and-adjustment-of-brake-pedal).)

## Brake Booster

*(Figure: brake booster component diagram — see page image p.745 (BR-16).)*

<a id="p746"></a>
### Removal of Brake Booster

1. **Remove master cylinder.** (See [Master Cylinder](#removal-of-master-cylinder).)
2. **Remove wheel guard.**
3. **Disconnect vacuum hose from brake booster.**
4. **Remove instrument lower finish panel and air duct.**
5. **Remove pedal return spring.**
6. **Remove clip and clevis pin.**
7. **Remove brake booster.**
   Remove the four nuts, and pull out the brake booster.

<a id="p747"></a>
### Installation of Brake Booster

1. **Adjust length of booster push rod.**
   1. Set SST on the master cylinder, and lower the pin until its tip slightly touches the
      piston.
      SST **09737-00010**
   2. Turn SST upside down, and set it on the booster.
      SST **09737-00010**
   3. Measure the clearance between the booster push rod and pin head (SST).
      Clearance: **0 mm (0 in.)**
   4. Adjust the booster push rod length until the push rod lightly touches the pin head.
2. **Install brake booster.**
   Install the brake booster, and torque the four nuts.
   Torque: **130 kg-cm (9 ft-lb, 13 N·m)**
3. **Connect clevis to brake pedal.**
   Insert the clevis pin into the clevis and brake pedal and install the clip to the clevis
   pin.
4. **Install pedal return spring.**
5. **Install wheel guard.**
6. **Install master cylinder.** (See [Master Cylinder](#installation-of-master-cylinder).)
7. **Connect hose to brake booster.**
8. **Fill brake reservoir with brake fluid and bleed brake system.**
   (See [Bleeding of Brake System](#bleeding-of-brake-system).)
9. **Check for leaks.**
10. **Check and adjust brake pedal.**
    (See [Check and Adjustment of Brake Pedal](#check-and-adjustment-of-brake-pedal).)
11. **Perform operational check.**
    (See [Operational Test of Brake Booster](#operational-test-of-brake-booster).)
12. **Install instrument lower finish panel and air duct.**
13. **Install luggage compartment trim cover.**

<a id="p749"></a>
### Replacement of Brake Pads

> **NOTE:** If a squealing noise occurs from the front brakes while driving, check the pad
> wear indicator plate. If there are traces of the indicator plate contacting the rotor
> disc, the brake pad should be replaced.

1. **Remove front wheel.**
   Remove the wheel and temporarily fasten the rotor disc with the hub nuts.
2. **Inspect pad lining thickness.**
   Check the pad thickness through the cylinder inspection hole and replace pads if not
   within specification.
   Minimum thickness: **3.0 mm (0.118 in.)**
3. **Remove cylinder from torque plate.**
   1. Remove two installation bolts from the torque plate.
   2. Remove the brake cylinder and suspend it so the hose is not stretched.
      > **NOTE:** Do not disconnect the brake hose.
4. **Remove following parts.**
   1. Two anti-squeal springs
   2. Two brake pads
   3. Four anti-squeal shims
   4. Two pad wear indicator plates
   5. Four pad support plates
5. **Check rotor disc thickness.** (See [step 2 under Inspection and Repair](#inspection-and-repair-of-front-brake-components).)
6. **Check rotor disc runout.** (See [step 3 under Inspection and Repair](#inspection-and-repair-of-front-brake-components).)
7. **Install pad support plates.**
8. **Install new pads.**
   1. Install a pad wear indicator plate to the pad.
      > **NOTE:** Be sure the arrow on the pad wear indicator plate is pointing in the
      > rotating direction of the disc.
   2. Install anti-squeal shims to the pad.
      > **NOTE:** Apply disc brake grease to both sides of the anti-squeal shim No. 3.
   3. Install the pads onto each support plate.
      > **CAUTION:** Do not allow oil or grease to get on the rubbing face.
   4. Install the two anti-squeal springs.
9. **Install cylinder.**
   1. Draw out a small amount of brake fluid from the reservoir.
   2. Press in piston with a hammer handle or an equivalent.
      > **NOTE:** Always change the pad on one wheel at a time as there is a possibility of
      > the opposite piston flying out.
   3. Insert the brake cylinder carefully so the boot is not wedged.
   4. Install and torque two installation bolts.
      Torque: **255 kg-cm (18 ft-lb, 25 N·m)**
10. **Install front wheel.**
11. **Check that fluid level is at MAX line.**

<a id="p752"></a>
### Removal of Cylinder

1. **Disconnect brake line.**
   Remove the union bolt and disconnect the brake line. Use a container to catch the brake
   fluid.
2. **Remove cylinder from torque plate.**
   Remove the two installation bolts and cylinder.
3. **Remove pads.** (See step 4 under [Replacement of Brake Pads](#replacement-of-brake-pads).)

<a id="p753"></a>
### Disassembly of Cylinder

1. **Remove following parts.**
   1. Two cylinder sliding bushings
   2. Four dust boots
   3. Two collars
2. **Remove cylinder boot set ring and cylinder boot.**
   Using a screwdriver, remove the cylinder boot set ring and cylinder boot.
3. **Remove piston from cylinder.**
   1. Put a piece of cloth or such between the piston and cylinder.
   2. Use compressed air to remove the piston from the cylinder.
   > **WARNING:** Do not place your fingers in front of the piston when using compressed
   > air.
4. **Remove piston seal from brake cylinder.**
   Using a screwdriver, remove the piston seal.

<a id="p754"></a>
### Inspection and Repair of Front Brake Components

1. **Measure pad lining thickness.**
   - Standard thickness: **10.0 mm (0.394 in.)**
   - Minimum thickness: **3.0 mm (0.118 in.)**

   Replace the pad if the thickness is less than the minimum or if it shows sign of uneven
   wear.
2. **Measure rotor disc thickness.**
   - Standard thickness: **22.0 mm (0.866 in.)**
   - Minimum thickness: **21.0 mm (0.827 in.)**

   If the disc is scored or worn, or if thickness is less than minimum, repair or replace
   the disc.
3. **Measure rotor disc runout.**
   > **NOTE:** Before measuring the runout, confirm that the front hub bearing play is
   > within specification.

   Measure the rotor disc runout at **10 mm (0.39 in.)** from the outer edge of the rotor
   disc.
   Maximum disc runout: **0.13 mm (0.0051 in.)**

   If the runout is greater than the maximum, inspect and adjust following the procedure
   below. Then replace the disc if necessary.
   1. Remove the torque plate from the knuckle.
   2. Remove the hub nuts of the temporarily installed disc and pull off the rotor disc.
   3. Check that the hub bearing play is within specification, and replace the bearing if
      not within specification. (See Front Suspension and Axle, page FA-8.)
   4. Install the rotor disc and measure the disc runout, then shift the rotor disc one
      fourth a turn and measure the disc runout. Similarly measure the runout at each
      position, and select the position where the runout is minimum.
   5. In this position, if the runout is within specification, install the torque plate and
      torque the mounting bolts.
      Torque: **900 kg-cm (65 ft-lb, 88 N·m)**
   6. If not within specification, replace the rotor disc, and repeat steps 4 and 5.

<a id="p755"></a>
### Assembly of Cylinder

1. **Apply lithium soap base glycol grease to parts indicated with arrows.**
2. **Install piston seal and piston in cylinder.**
3. **Install cylinder boot and set ring in cylinder.**
4. **Install collar, dust boot and cylinder sliding bushing.**
   1. Install the collar and dust boot into the brake cylinder.
   2. Ensure that the boot is secured firmly to the brake cylinder groove.
   3. Install the bushing into the boot.
   4. Ensure that the boot is secured firmly to the bushing groove.

<a id="p756"></a>
### Installation of Cylinder

1. **Install pads.** (See steps 7 and 8 under [Replacement of Brake Pads](#replacement-of-brake-pads).)
2. **Install cylinder.** (See step 9 under [Replacement of Brake Pads](#replacement-of-brake-pads).)
3. **Install flexible hose to brake cylinder.**
   Set the flexible hose and new gaskets in position and install the union bolt.
   Torque: **310 kg-cm (22 ft-lb, 30 N·m)**
4. **Fill brake reservoir with brake fluid and bleed brake system.**
   (See [Bleeding of Brake System](#bleeding-of-brake-system).)
5. **Check for leaks.**

### Replacement of Brake Pads

> **NOTE:** If a squealing noise occurs from the rear brakes while driving, check the pad

1. **Remove rear wheel.**
   Remove the wheel and temporarily fasten the rotor disc with the hub nuts.
2. **Inspect pad lining thickness.**
   Check the pad thickness through the cylinder inspection hole and replace the pads if they
   are not within specification.
   Minimum thickness: **1.0 mm (0.039 in.)**
3. **Draw out a small amount of brake fluid.**
4. **Remove cylinder from torque plate.**
   1. Remove the installation bolt from the torque plate.
   2. Lift up the brake cylinder and suspend it.
      > **NOTE:** Do not remove the cylinder from the main pin, and do not disconnect the
      > brake line.
5. **Remove following parts:**
   1. Two brake pads
   2. Two anti-squeal shims
   3. Two anti-rattle springs
   4. Pad support plate
   5. Two pad guide plates
6. **Check rotor disc thickness.** (See [step 2 under Inspection](#inspection-of-rear-brake-components).)
7. **Check rotor disc runout.** (See [step 3 under Inspection](#inspection-of-rear-brake-components).)
8. **Install pad support plate, anti-rattle springs and pad guide plates.**
9. **Install new pads and anti-squeal shims.**
   1. Install the anti-squeal shims to the pads.
   2. Raise the cylinder, and install the pads to the cylinder mounting.
      > **CAUTION:** Do not allow oil or grease to get on the rubbing face.
10. **Lower cylinder.**
    1. Using SST, slowly turn the piston clockwise while pushing it in until it locks.
       SST **09719-14020 (09719-00020)**
    2. Fit the pad protrusion into the piston stopper groove, and install the cylinder.
       > **NOTE:** Insert the cylinder carefully so the boot is not wedged.
11. **Install cylinder installation bolt.**
    Install and torque the cylinder installation bolt.
    Torque: **200 kg-cm (14 ft-lb, 20 N·m)**
12. **Install rear wheel.**
13. **Check that fluid level is at "MAX" line.**
14. **Adjust parking brake automatic adjuster by depressing brake pedal several times.**

<a id="p760"></a>
### Removal of Cylinder

1. **Disconnect brake hose from cylinder.**
   1. Remove the union bolt and two gaskets, and disconnect the brake hose.
   2. Use a container to catch the brake fluid.
2. **Disconnect parking brake cable.**
   1. Remove the pin clip.
   2. Pull out the hole pin while pushing the parking brake cable.
   3. Remove the clip.
   4. Remove the parking brake cable from the cable support bracket.
3. **Remove cylinder.**
   Remove the installation bolt, and remove the brake cylinder.
4. **Remove pads.** (See step 5 under [Replacement of Brake Pads](#replacement-of-brake-pads).)

<a id="p761"></a>
### Disassembly of Cylinder

1. **Remove sliding bushing and boot.**
2. **Remove main pin boot.**
3. **Remove cylinder boot set ring and cylinder boot.**
   Using a screwdriver, remove the cylinder boot set ring and cylinder boot.
4. **Remove piston from cylinder.**
   Using SST, turn the piston counterclockwise and remove it.
   SST **09719-14020 (09719-00020)**
5. **Remove piston seal from cylinder.**
   Using a screwdriver, remove the piston seal.
6. **Remove snap ring from cylinder.**
   1. Set SST onto the adjusting bolt, and lightly tighten it with a 14 mm socket.
      SST **09756-00010**
      > **CAUTION:**
      > - To ensure safety, always use SST as there is a possibility of the spring flying
      >   out, causing injury or damage to the interior surface of the cylinder.
      > - Be careful not to tighten the SST too tightly as this may damage the spring
      >   retainer.
   2. Using snap ring pliers, remove the snap ring from the cylinder.
7. **Remove adjusting bolt.**
   From the cylinder, pull out the spring retainer, spring, spring plate and stopper
   together with the adjusting bolt.
   > **CAUTION:**
   > - Be careful not to pry too hard.
   > - Be careful not to damage the O-ring.
8. **Disassemble adjusting bolt.**
   1. Remove SST.
      SST **09756-00010**
   2. Remove the spring retainer, spring, spring plate and stopper from the adjusting bolt.
   3. Remove the O-ring from the adjusting bolt.
9. **Remove strut.**
10. **Remove torsion spring from parking brake crank.**
11. **Remove parking brake crank from cylinder.**
12. **Remove parking brake crank boot.**
    Using a screwdriver, lightly tap on the metal portion of the boot and remove it.
    > **NOTE:** Do not remove the boot unless replacing it.
13. **Remove cable support bracket.**
14. **Remove stopper pin.**
    Using a pin punch, tap out the pin.

<a id="p764"></a>
### Inspection of Rear Brake Components

1. **Measure pad lining thickness.**
   - Standard thickness: **10.0 mm (0.394 in.)**
   - Minimum thickness: **1.0 mm (0.039 in.)**

   Replace the pad if the thickness is less than the minimum or if it shows sign of uneven
   wear.
2. **Measure rotor disc thickness.**
   - Standard thickness: **10.0 mm (0.394 in.)**
   - Minimum thickness: **9.0 mm (0.354 in.)**

   If the disc is scored or worn, or if thickness is less than minimum, repair or replace
   the disc.
3. **Measure rotor disc runout.**
   Measure the rotor disc runout at **10 mm (0.39 in.)** from the outer edge of the rotor
   disc.
   Maximum disc runout: **0.15 mm (0.0059 in.)**

   If the runout is greater than the maximum, replace the disc.
4. **If necessary, replace rotor disc.**
   1. Remove the disc brake cylinder mounting from the dust cover.
   2. Remove the hub nuts and rotor disc.
   3. Install a new rotor disc and temporarily fasten the disc with hub nuts.
   4. Install the torque plate to the rear axle carrier.
      Torque: **600 kg-cm (43 ft-lb, 59 N·m)**

<a id="p765"></a>
### Assembly of Cylinder

1. **Apply lithium soap base glycol grease to parts indicated by arrows.**
   > **NOTE:** Pack the lithium soap base glycol grease into areas marked "A".
2. **Install stopper pin.**
   Tap in the pin to the brake cylinder until the stopper pin extends **25 mm (0.98 in.)**.
3. **Install cable support bracket.**
   Press the surface of the support bracket flush against the cylinder wall and tighten the
   bolt.
   Torque: **475 kg-cm (34 ft-lb, 47 N·m)**
4. **Install parking brake crank boot in cylinder.**
5. **Install parking brake crank in cylinder.**
   > **CAUTION:** Securely match the crank boot with the groove of the crank seal.
6. **Check that there is clearance as shown in figure.**
7. **Install torsion spring.**
   Install the torsion spring and check that the parking brake crank subassembly is in
   touch with the stopper pin.
8. **Install strut.**
   Before installing the strut, adjust the rollers of the needle roller bearing so they do
   not catch on the cylinder hole.
9. **Install a new O-ring to adjusting bolt.**
10. **Assemble adjusting bolt.**
    1. Assemble the stopper, plate, spring and spring retainer to the adjusting bolt, and
       using SST, fully tighten them down by hand.
       SST **09756-00010**
       > **CAUTION:**
       > - Position the inscribed surface of the stopper upward.
       > - Align the notches of the spring case with the notches of the stopper.
    2. Install the adjusting bolt subassembly into the cylinder.
11. **Install snap ring.**
    1. Using snap ring pliers, install the snap ring.
       > **NOTE:** Face the snap ring opening toward the bleeder side.
    2. Remove the SST.
       SST **09756-00010**
    3. Firmly pull up the adjusting bolt by hand and ensure that it does not move.
12. **Perform operational check.**
    After performing steps 1 to 11, move the parking brake crank by hand and ensure that
    the adjusting bolt moves smoothly.
13. **Install piston seal in cylinder.**
14. **Assemble piston in cylinder.**
    1. Using SST, slowly screw in the piston clockwise until it will not descend any
       further.
       SST **09719-14020 (09719-00020)**
    2. Align the center of the piston stopper groove with the positioning protrusion of the
       cylinder.
15. **Install cylinder boot and set ring in cylinder.**
16. **Install main pin boot.**
    Using a 21 mm socket wrench, press in a new boot.
17. **Install dust boot and sliding bushing.**
    1. Install the dust boot.
       > **NOTE:** Be careful that the seal does not fold under.
    2. Install the bushing into the boot, with the flange facing inside.

<a id="p769"></a>
### Installation of Cylinder

1. **Install pad support plate, anti-rattle springs and pad guide plates.**
   (See step 8 under [Replacement of Brake Pads](#replacement-of-brake-pads).)
2. **Install pads and anti-squeal shims.**
   1. Install the anti-squeal shims to the pads.
   2. Install the pads to the cylinder mounting.
      > **CAUTION:** Do not allow oil or grease to get on the rubbing face.
3. **Install cylinder.**
   1. Install the cylinder onto the main pin.
      > **NOTE:** Make sure that the boot end is installed into the groove of the main pin.
   2. Fit the pad protrusion into the piston stopper groove, and install the cylinder.
      > **NOTE:** Insert the cylinder carefully so the boot is not wedged.
4. **Install cylinder installation bolt.**
   Install the cylinder installation bolt and torque the bolt.
   Torque: **200 kg-cm (14 ft-lb, 20 N·m)**
5. **Connect parking brake cable.**
   1. Install the parking brake cable to the cable support bracket and install the retainer.
   2. Install the hole pin while pushing the parking brake lever, and install the new clip.
6. **Connect brake hose.**
   Install the brake hose and new gaskets in position, and tighten the union bolt.
   Torque: **310 kg-cm (22 ft-lb, 30 N·m)**
7. **Fill brake reservoir with brake fluid and bleed brake system.**
   (See [Bleeding of Brake System](#bleeding-of-brake-system).)
8. **Check for fluid leakage.**
9. **Adjust rear brake.**
   Depress the brake pedal several times and adjust the rear brake automatically.

## Brake Hoses and Tubes

<a id="p771"></a>
### Disconnect and Connect Hose and Tube

1. **Disconnect hose and tube.**
   1. Remove the clip.
   2. Using a wrench to hold the hose and SST to hold the tube, disconnect the tube and
      hose.
   SST **09751-36011**
2. **Connect hose and tube.**
   1. Connect the hose and tube by hand.
   2. Using a wrench to hold the hose and SST to hold the tube, torque the connection.
      SST **09751-36011**
      Torque: **155 kg-cm (11 ft-lb, 15 N·m)**
   3. Install a hose clip.

<a id="p771"></a>
### Inspection of Brake Hoses and Tubes

1. **Inspect brake hoses.**
   1. Inspect the hose for damage, cracks or swelling.
   2. Inspect the threads for damage.
2. **Inspect brake tubes.**
   1. Inspect the tube for damage, cracks, dents or corrosion.
   2. Inspect the threads for damage.
