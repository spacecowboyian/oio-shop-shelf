## 1  General information

The vehicles covered by this manual are equipped with either a five-speed manual or a three or four-speed automatic transaxle. Information on the manual transaxle is in this Part of Chapter 7. Service procedures for the automatic transaxle are in [Chapter 7, Part B](07b-automatic-transaxle.md).

The manual transaxle is a compact, two-piece, lightweight aluminum alloy housing containing both the transmission and differential assemblies. Because of the complexity, unavailability of replacement parts and special tools necessary, internal repair procedures for the manual transaxle are not recommended for the home mechanic.

The bulk of information in this Chapter is devoted to removal and installation procedures.

## 2  Oil seal - replacement

Oil leaks frequently occur due to wear of the differential side gear shaft seals (on models with flanged inner CV joints) or the driveaxle seals (on models with splined inner CV joints), and/or the speedometer driven gear oil seal and O-rings. Replacement of these seals is relatively easy, since the repairs can usually be performed without removing the transaxle from the vehicle.

### Driveaxle seals

1. These seals are located at the sides of the transaxle, where the side gear shafts (or driveaxles, on models with splined inner CV joints) are attached. If leakage at the seal is suspected, raise the vehicle and support it securely on jackstands. If the seal is leaking, lubricant will be found on the side of the transaxle.
2. Refer to [Chapter 8](08-clutch-and-driveaxles.md) and remove the driveaxles (and side gear shafts on models with flanged inner CV joints).
3. Using a screwdriver or pry bar, carefully pry the oil seal out of the transaxle bore (see [Chapter 7, Part B](07b-automatic-transaxle.md)).
4. If the oil seal cannot be removed with a screwdriver or pry bar, a special oil seal removal tool (available at auto parts stores) will be required.
5. Using a large section of pipe or a large deep socket as a drift, install the new oil seal. Drive it into the bore squarely and make sure it's completely seated. Lubricate the lip of the new seal with multi-purpose grease.
6. Install the side gear shaft(s) (if equipped) and the driveaxle(s). Be careful not to damage the lip of the new seal.

### Speedometer cable O-ring

*Refer to illustrations 2.9 and 2.10.*

7. The speedometer cable and driven gear housing are located on the transaxle near the shift cable linkage. Look for lubricant around the cable housing to determine if the seal and O-ring are leaking.
8. Disconnect the speedometer cable and remove the driven gear housing from the transaxle (see illustration).

   <!-- DIAGRAM CANDIDATE: p.172 — Illustration 2.9: using a pair of pliers to loosen the speedometer cable fitting -->

9. Using a hook, remove the seal from the driven gear housing (see illustration).

   <!-- DIAGRAM CANDIDATE: p.173 — Illustration 2.10: using a hooked tool to pull the old speedometer cable seal out of the driven gear housing (use a small hammer and socket to tap the new seal into place; make sure the seal is recessed the same amount) -->

10. Using a small socket of the appropriate diameter or other similar tool as a drift, install the new seal.
11. Install a new O-ring in the driven gear housing and reattach the speedometer cable to the housing.

## 4  Shift cables - removal and installation

1. Detach the cable from the negative battery terminal.

### 1988 and earlier models

*Refer to illustration 4.2.*

2. Remove the retaining clips and disconnect the shift cables from the transaxle linkage (see illustration).

   <!-- DIAGRAM CANDIDATE: p.173 — Illustration 4.2: shift cable/linkage details — 1 Cable retainers, 2 Shift control cable, 3 Shift select cable, 4 Retaining clips, 5 Cable and washer -->

3. Remove the console and shifter boot.
4. Remove the cable retainers from the shift lever assembly (see illustration 3.3).
5. Disconnect the shift cables from the shift lever assembly (see illustration 3.3).
6. Remove the left front sill plate and pull the carpet back to gain access to the cables.
7. Remove the shift cable retainer screws at the floor pan and detach the cables.

### 1989 and later models

8. Remove the shift lever knob and boot.
9. Remove the front and rear center console halves.
10. Remove the center air duct.
11. Remove the ECM-to-floor mounting nuts and remove the ECM from under the dash.
12. Remove the shift cable hold-down bracket.
13. Disconnect the shift cables from the control assembly (see illustration 3.3).
14. Remove the shift cable retainers at the transaxle and disconnect the shift cables from the transaxle linkage (see illustration 4.2).
15. Pull the shift cables through from the inside of the vehicle to remove them.

### All models

16. Installation is the reverse of removal.

## 5  Transaxle mounts - check and replacement

*Refer to illustration 5.1.*

1. Insert a large screwdriver or pry bar between each mount and the transaxle and pry up while watching the mount (see illustration).

   <!-- DIAGRAM CANDIDATE: p.174 — Illustration 5.1: transaxle mount locations, typical — 1 Transaxle front mount, 2 Transaxle rear mount, 3 Main crossmember, 4 Center crossmember, 5 Transaxle center mount, 6 Mount bolt shields -->

2. If the rubber separates from the metal plate on the mount, or the case moves up, but not down (mount bottomed out), replace the mount.
3. To replace a mount, support the transaxle with a jack, remove the nuts and bolts and detach the mount. It may be necessary to raise the transaxle slightly to provide enough clearance to remove the mount.
4. Installation is the reverse of removal.

<a id="p174-manual-transaxle-removal"></a>
## 7  Manual transaxle - removal and installation

### 1987 and earlier models

*Refer to illustrations 7.9, 7.10, 7.14 and 7.15.*

1. Detach the negative cable from the battery.
2. Remove the air cleaner and inlet tube (see [Chapter 4](04-fuel-and-exhaust-systems.md)).
3. Unplug the back-up light switch wire harness.
4. Disconnect the speedometer cable (see illustration 2.9).
5. Disconnect the shift cables (see [Section 4](#p173-shift-cables)).
6. If the vehicle has a thermostat housing on the transaxle bellhousing, detach it. If it is necessary to disconnect coolant hoses, refer to [Chapter 3](03-cooling-heating-and-air-conditioning.md) for cooling system draining procedures.
7. Remove the clutch slave cylinder (see [Chapter 8](08-clutch-and-driveaxles.md)) and set it aside.
8. Remove the cover from under the transaxle.
9. Detach the mounts from the center crossmember (see illustration).

   <!-- DIAGRAM CANDIDATE: p.174 — Illustration 7.9: detach the center mounts from the crossmember ... -->

10. Unbolt the center crossmember and take it out (see illustration).

    <!-- DIAGRAM CANDIDATE: p.174 — Illustration 7.10: ... then remove the crossmember from the vehicle -->

11. Disconnect both driveaxles from the transaxle. Remove the left driveaxle from the vehicle (see [Chapter 8](08-clutch-and-driveaxles.md)).
12. Remove the starter (see [Chapter 5](05-engine-electrical-systems.md)).
13. Disconnect the ground strap.
14. Remove the bellhousing lower cover plate (see illustration).

    <!-- DIAGRAM CANDIDATE: p.175 — Illustration 7.14: the lower cover plate (arrow) is bolted to the bellhousing -->

15. Support the engine and transaxle with jacks (one each for the engine and transaxle) (see illustration). Use a block of wood beneath the jack and engine oil pan to protect the oil pan. If an engine hoist is available, use it, instead of the jack, to support the engine. Transmission jacks, which can be rented, are the best to use beneath the transaxle. They have cradles and chains to keep the transaxle from falling.

    <!-- DIAGRAM CANDIDATE: p.175 — Illustration 7.15: slowly lower the jacks beneath the engine and transaxle -->

16. Raise the jacks just enough to remove tension from the left engine mount, then remove the mount.

    > **CAUTION:** The engine must remain supported at all times when the transaxle is out of the vehicle!

17. Remove the transaxle-to-engine bolts.
18. Make a final check that all wires, hoses and linkage have been disconnected from the transaxle.
19. Slowly lower the jacks beneath the engine and transaxle. Lower the left side of the engine to provide removal clearance for the transaxle. Separate the transaxle from the engine and roll it out from under the vehicle. If you didn't use a transmission jack, make sure the transaxle remains balanced on the jack - don't let it tip to one side or it'll slide off.

    > **CAUTION:** Don't depress the clutch pedal while the transaxle is out of the vehicle.

20. Inspect the clutch components (see [Chapter 8](08-clutch-and-driveaxles.md)). It's a good idea to install new clutch components whenever the transaxle is removed.
21. Installation is the reverse of removal. Be sure to tighten the transaxle-to-engine bolts to the torque listed in this Chapter's Specifications.

### 1988 and later models

22. Remove the battery and battery tray from the vehicle (see [Chapter 5](05-engine-electrical-systems.md)).
23. Remove the air cleaner assembly (see [Chapter 4](04-fuel-and-exhaust-systems.md)).
24. Unplug the back-up switch electrical connector.
25. Detach the ground strap.
26. Remove the two clutch release cylinder mounting bolts.
27. Detach the release cylinder hydraulic line bracket.
28. Detach the shift cable retainers and end clips.
29. Detach the shift cables from the brackets and lay them aside.
30. Remove the left transaxle mount cover and brace.
31. Support the transaxle with a jack. Remove the left transaxle mount through bolt.
32. Remove the two upper transaxle-to-engine bolts.
33. Remove the upper bolt from the starter motor (see [Chapter 5](05-engine-electrical-systems.md)).
34. Detach the speedometer cable (see illustration 2.9).
35. Attach an engine hoist to the engine.
36. Loosen the front wheel lug nuts, then raise the vehicle and place it securely on jackstands.
37. Remove both front wheels.
38. Remove the left and right splash shields.
39. Detach the starter electrical connections (see [Chapter 5](05-engine-electrical-systems.md)).
40. Remove both driveaxles (see [Chapter 8](08-clutch-and-driveaxles.md)).
41. Remove the three center crossmember-to-radiator support bolts.
42. Remove the front and center mount bolt shields (see illustration 5.1).
43. Remove the two front mount nuts.

    > **NOTE:** Mount nuts may be of different thicknesses to accommodate different stud lengths. Label the nuts so they can be returned to their original locations.

44. Remove the two center mount nuts.
45. Remove the two rear mount nuts.
46. Remove the two center crossmember-to-main crossmember bolts.
47. Remove the three exhaust hanger bracket nuts and exhaust hanger (see [Chapter 4](04-fuel-and-exhaust-systems.md)).
48. Support the main crossmember with a floor jack.
49. Remove the main crossmember-to-underbody bolts.
50. Remove the two lower control arm bracket-to-underbody bolts.
51. Lower the main crossmember slowly while holding onto the center crossmember.

    > **WARNING:** The center crossmember is free to fall at this time and could cause serious injury.

52. Remove the front mount through-bolt and mount.
53. Remove the front mount bracket from the transaxle.
54. Remove the center mount from the transaxle.
55. Remove the clutch inspection cover.
56. Remove the two lower transaxle bracket-to-mount nuts.
57. Lower the vehicle.
58. Remove the remaining transaxle mount-to-bracket bolt.
59. Lower the engine/transaxle assembly enough to provide clearance to remove the transaxle.
60. Remove the transaxle mount.
61. Raise the vehicle again and support it securely on jackstands.
62. Support the transaxle with a floor jack. Place a wood block between the jack and transaxle pan to protect the pan from damage.
63. Remove the front and rear lower transaxle-to-engine bolts.
64. Remove the remaining transaxle mount-to-bracket bolt.
65. Make a final check that all wires and hoses have been disconnected from the transaxle.
66. Move the transaxle and jack toward the side of the vehicle until the transaxle is clear of the engine. Make sure the transaxle remains balanced on the jack - don't let it tip to one side or it'll slide off.
67. Carefully lower the transaxle and remove it from under the vehicle.

    > **CAUTION:** Don't depress the clutch pedal while the transaxle is out of the vehicle.

68. Inspect the clutch components (see [Chapter 8](08-clutch-and-driveaxles.md)). It's a good idea to install new clutch components whenever the transaxle is removed.
69. Installation is the reverse of removal.

## 8  Manual transaxle overhaul - general information

Overhauling a manual transaxle is a difficult job for the do-it-yourselfer. It involves the disassembly and reassembly of many small parts. Numerous clearances must be precisely measured and, if necessary, changed with select fit spacers and snap-rings. As a result, if transaxle problems arise, it can be removed and installed by a competent do-it-yourselfer, but overhaul should be left to a transmission repair shop. Rebuilt transaxles may be available - check with a dealer parts department and auto parts stores.
