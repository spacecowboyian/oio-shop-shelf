### Oil Pump — Inspection

All values below **image-verified** against the page.

1. Measure the **tip clearance**. If it exceeds the limit, replace the oil pump drive rotors.

   | | Value |
   | --- | --- |
   | Limit | 0.2 mm (0.008 in) |
   | Standard | 0.10–0.15 mm (0.0039–0.0059 in) |

2. Measure the **side clearance** (between rotor and cover). If it exceeds the limit, replace either rotor or pump body.

   | | Value |
   | --- | --- |
   | Limit | 0.15 mm (0.0059 in) |
   | Standard | 0.03–0.07 mm (0.0012–0.0028 in) |

3. Measure the **body clearance** (between driven rotor and pump body). If it exceeds the limit, replace either rotor or pump body.

   | | Value |
   | --- | --- |
   | Limit | 0.2 mm (0.008 in) |
   | Standard | 0.10–0.16 mm (0.0039–0.0063 in) |

4. Inspect the **relief valve** for scoring and wear. If damaged, replace the valve or pump assembly.

### Oil Pump — Check pump operation

Immerse the suction end of the pump into clean engine oil and turn the shaft clockwise with
a screwdriver. Oil should come out of the discharge hole. Close off the discharge hole with
your thumb and turn the shaft as before — the shaft should be difficult to turn.
