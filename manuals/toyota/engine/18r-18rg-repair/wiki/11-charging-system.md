### Charging System Circuit — standard (contact-point regulator)

Charging-circuit wiring for the alternator with an external contact-point voltage
regulator. *(Figs. 11-1 and 11-2 — wiring pictorial and schematic; see the source page
image.)* The circuit shows the alternator (stator coil, rotor coil, slip ring and brush),
the regulator (voltage regulator coil, voltage relay coil), the charge warning light, and
the ignition switch / fusible link.

- Engine fuse: **15A**
- Heater fuse: **20A**

### Performance Test by Alternator Checker

SST for all steps below: **[09081-00011]**.

1. Unplug the alternator regulator connector and plug in the checker connector.
   Push **"20 V"** switch. *(Fig. 11-17)*
2. Check **"B" terminal voltage**. Push **"B"** switch. Raise engine speed from idling to
   2000 rpm. *(Fig. 11-18)*
   - Standard voltage: **13.8 – 14.8 V**
3. Check **"F" terminal voltage**. Push **"F"** switch. Gradually raise engine speed. The
   checker reading should gradually decrease from **12 volt to 3 volt**. *(Fig. 11-19)*
4. Check **"N" terminal voltage**. Push **"N"** switch. Maintain engine speed at approx.
   **1500 rpm**. The pointer should be at a half of "B" terminal voltage. *(Fig. 11-20)*

### Alternator Inspection (on vehicle)

1. **Negative side rectifier short test.** *(Fig. 11-21)* Connect an ohmmeter (−) lead to N
   terminal and (+) lead to E terminal. Meter should indicate infinity.
2. **Positive side rectifier short test.** *(Fig. 11-22)* Connect an ohmmeter (−) lead to B
   terminal and (+) lead to N terminal. Meter should indicate infinity.
3. **Check rotor coil resistance.** *(Fig. 11-23)* — Resistance **5 – 9 Ω**
4. **Field circuit check.** *(Fig. 11-24)* Turn ignition switch to ON position, and check
   if there is battery voltage at F terminal. If not, check ENGINE fuse.

### Inspection and Repair — Rectifier (standard)

1. **Rectifier holder positive side.** *(Fig. 11-41)* Connect an ohmmeter (+) lead to the
   rectifier holder, and the (−) lead of the meter to the rectifier terminal. If there is
   no continuity, rectifier assembly must be replaced.
2. **Reverse polarity** of test leads and check again. *(Fig. 11-42)* If there is
   continuity, rectifier assembly must be replaced.
3. **Rectifier holder negative side.** *(Fig. 11-43)* Connect an ohmmeter (+) lead to the
   rectifier terminal, and the (−) lead of the meter to the rectifier holder. If there is
   no continuity, rectifier assembly must be replaced.
4. **Reverse polarity** of test leads and check again. *(Fig. 11-44)* If there is
   continuity, rectifier assembly must be replaced.

### Assembly steps

1. Insert insulator between positive rectifier holder and brush holder. *(Fig. 11-58)*
2. Install brush holder onto rectifier holder using socket wrench and a vise. *(Fig. 11-59)*
3. Connect stator coil "N" lead onto brush holder terminal, and solder each stator lead and
   rectifier lead to positive rectifier. *(Fig. 11-60)*

   > **[with IC regulator]** Solder each lead wire onto rectifier or terminal as is shown in
   > the figure. *(Fig. 11-61)*

---
<a id="p308"></a>
**[PDF p.308]** — ALTERNATOR / ASSEMBLY (steps, cont.) · printed page 11-24

4. Assemble rear end frame and rectifier holder with insulators. *(Fig. 11-62)*
5. Assemble rear end cover with insulators. *(Fig. 11-63)*

   > **Caution:** If there is danger of stator coil terminal wiring contacting on frame or
   > rotor, correct by bending wiring. *(Fig. 11-64)*

6. Install rear bearing facing its sealed side forward. *(Fig. 11-65)*
