## Removing and refitting a half shaft tube

### Right-hand side

<!-- PDF p.308 · J-4 -->

![Half shaft tube — removal (arrows to the strap, shock-absorber points and half-shell nuts) and the checking set-up: the tube on gauge TAr.56A with dimension D between each trunnion and the gauge — PDF p.308](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0308-half-shaft-tube-removal-checking.webp)

**Removing:**

1. Remove the wheel, the drum and the half shaft.
2. Remove the brake anchor plate (fasten it in order to avoid tension being applied to the brake hose).
3. Compress the suspension spring using claws **Sus.21**.
4. Disconnect the top end of the shock absorber.
5. Disconnect the strap and allow the half shaft tube to come down.
6. Unscrew the lower shock absorber attachment point and remove it.
7. Remove the spring.
8. Lift the fuel tank and separate it from its mounting lug.
9. Remove the side mounting pad and its bracket.
10. Mark the positions of the half shells with respect to the differential carrier.
11. Remove the half shell securing nuts.
12. Remove the half shaft tube (take care with the needle track rings). Separate the half shells.

**Checking:**

1. Check that the half shaft tube is not bent and that there are no scores on the trunnions.
2. Place the half shaft tube on gauge **TAr.56A** and secure it by two hollow hexagon bolts.
3. Check dimension **D** with a set of feeler gauges. This is the dimension between each trunnion and
   the gauge. The difference between these two figures should not exceed **0.2 mm (.008")**.
4. If this is not the case, the half shaft tube is to be replaced.

**Refitting:**

Carry out the removing operations in reverse.

> **NOTE:** Fit the spacer washers on the half shells at the point where the side mounting pad is
> secured. Half shell securing nut tightening torque: **5 m.kg (35 lbs.ft)**.

### Left-hand side

**Removing:**

This is carried out in the same way as for removing the right hand half shaft tube (in addition,
remove the clutch and accelerator cable cover end stops).

**Checking:**

This is carried out in the same way as for the right hand half shaft tube.

**Refitting:**

This is carried out in the same way as for the right hand half shaft tube.

> **NOTE:** Fit the spacer washers to the half shells at the point to which the side mounting pad and
> the cable cover bracket are secured.

## Replacing the two gearbox side mounting pads

<!-- PDF p.309 · J-5 -->

### Right-hand pad

1. Take the weight of the fuel tank and separate it from its mounting lug.
2. Remove the pad together with its bracket.
3. Separate the pad from its bracket.
4. Fit the new pad to the bracket.
5. Fit the pad bracket (tightening torque for the nuts on the half shells **5 m.kg (35 lbs.ft)**).
6. Fit the earth braid and secure the fuel tank.

<!-- PDF p.310 · J-6 -->

![Left-hand mounting pad (photo, arrows to the securing points) and the rear-axle universal joint exploded view — closed yoke type — PDF p.310](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0310-mounting-pad-universal-joint.webp)

### Left-hand pad

1. Remove the clutch and accelerator cable cover bracket.
2. Remove the pad together with its bracket.
3. Separate the pad from the bracket.
4. Fit the new pad to the bracket.
5. Fit the pad bracket and the cable cover bracket.
6. Tightening torque for the nuts on the half shells: **5 m.kg (35 lbs.ft)**.

## Universal joint

<!-- PDF p.310 · J-6 -->

The old type universal joint fitted up to **1962** is no longer supplied by the C.S.S.

In the case of damage, it is to be replaced by the closed yoke type. This later type is supplied with
either **10 or 20 splines** and may be fitted to all the various gearbox (transmission case) types.

> **NOTE:** Certain of the illustrations used in this chapter show vehicles fitted with aerostable
> suspension. The operations involved in repairing vehicles that are not fitted with this type of
> suspension remain identical.
