## I — Renault heater

<!-- PDF p.438 · N-3 -->

![Renault heater — installation in the body (hot air trapped in the radiator casings and ducted to the front nozzles and the rear seat base) and the heater/radiator unit — PDF p.438](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0438-renault-heater-installation.webp)

Heating and demisting are achieved by hot air which has passed through the radiator. This
air is trapped in the casings which cover the radiator and ducted into the car:

- One of the ducts flows out under the rear seat base (heating).
- Two ducts distribute the air at the front (heating and demisting).

The efficiency of these two devices depends on the temperature of the water in the radiator
(minimum **80°C (176°F)**) and consequently on the position of the radiator blind.

## II — Standard SOFICA heater (6 V, 12 V)

### Description (standard SOFICA)

<!-- PDF p.439 · N-4 -->

![Standard SOFICA heater — installation in the body, and heater cutaway showing radiator (R), filter screen (F) and fan (V) — PDF p.439](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0439-standard-sofica-installation.webp)

The functions of heating and demisting are carried out by hot air supplied by the SOFICA
heater. The installation consists of:

- A SOFICA heater in the engine compartment.
- A duct which connects the heater to the control casing.
- A control casing under the scuttle.
- Two ducts connecting the control casing to the nozzles.
- Two demisting nozzles.

### Principle of operation (standard SOFICA)

<!-- PDF p.439 · N-4 -->

The SOFICA heater consists of:

- A radiator (R).
- A filter screen (F).
- A fan (V).

The fan (V) is operated by a switch mounted on the dashboard support and sucks air through
radiator (R) in the heater, which is heated as it flows through, forcing it into the control
casing.

### Thermostat — specifications and checking (standard SOFICA)

<!-- PDF p.440 · N-5 -->

**Thermostat: 26 — Reference 794.** <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: the scan's left margin is cropped here; the same thermostat is labelled "CL 26 — Reference 794" on N-11, so a "CL" prefix is likely present but not legible on this page. -->

![Cooling-system thermostat — sectioned views closed and open (water-flow arrows) and the engine-compartment installation — PDF p.440](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0440-thermostat-section.webp)

So that the heating system comes into operation quickly a thermostat is fitted in the engine
cooling system between the cylinder head and the engine radiator. The thermostat prevents
the water flowing to the engine radiator until its temperature reaches **82°C (180°F)** (the
temperature at which the thermostat opens). During the time that it is closed the quantity
of water in circulation is thus reduced and heats up more rapidly.

**Specifications:**

| Item | Value |
| ---- | ----- |
| Starts to open at | 82°C (180°F) |
| Fully opened at | 92°C (198°F) |

**Checking:**

1. Place the thermostat in a container in which there is cold water, with its valve
   uppermost.
2. Heat the water whilst keeping it moving.
3. When the water temperature reaches **82°C (180°F)** the valve should start to open.
4. At **92°C (198°F)** the valve should be **6.5 mm (1/4")** open, at least.

> If it does not open at the correct time and by the correct amount, replace the thermostat.

### Removing and refitting the SOFICA heater (standard)

<!-- PDF p.441 · N-6 -->

![Standard SOFICA heater removal — input leads (1), earth/ground lead (2), securing bolt (3), duct (4); and the heater-motor exploded view (filter screen F, fan V, motor M, casing C, radiator R) — PDF p.441](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0441-sofica-heater-removal.webp)

**Removing:**

1. Disconnect the battery.
2. Disconnect the input leads (1) and the earth (ground) lead (2).
3. Remove the heater securing bolt (3).
4. Disconnect the duct (4) which connects the heater to its control casing.

**Refitting:** Carry out the removing operations in reverse.

### Removing and refitting the heater motor

<!-- PDF p.441 · N-6 -->

**Removing:**

1. Remove the filter screen (F).
2. Remove the casing (C) (press on it lightly in order to remove it).
3. Remove the motor fan unit.

Separate motor (M) from fan (V), to carry out the operations involved in cleaning the
commutator and replacing the brushes (soldered to the leads).

<!-- PDF p.442 · N-7 -->

**Refitting:** Carry out the removing operations in reverse. Place the motor securing
components in their correct positions.

### Removing and refitting the heater radiator

<!-- PDF p.442 · N-7 -->

![Heater-radiator removal — drain plug (5), hoses (6) and (7) and the radiator securing bolts; plus the bleeding and filter-screen maintenance views — PDF p.442](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0442-heater-radiator-removal.webp)

**Removing:**

1. Drain the engine and heater radiator. (Drain plug on the lower right hand side (5).)
2. Disconnect the two hoses (6) and (7).
3. Remove the radiator securing bolts.

**Refitting:** Carry out the removing operations in reverse.

### Bleeding

<!-- PDF p.442 · N-7 -->

1. Unscrew the bleed screw on the heater radiator (top right hand side).
2. Refill the cooling system until water runs from the SOFICA heater radiator.
3. Screw the bleed screw in again and top-up the system.

### Maintenance (standard SOFICA)

<!-- PDF p.442 · N-7 -->

Every **5,000 km (3,000 miles)** remove the filter screen, clean it with petrol (gasoline),
dry it with compressed air and spray lubri-filtre compound on it.

### Heater control casing

<!-- PDF p.443 · N-8 -->

The air is distributed (to either heating or demisting) by means of two control rods
(heating and demisting) placed on either side of the control casing:

- C (heating)
- D (demisting)

**Removing the heater control casing:**

1. Remove the boot (trunk) lining and unscrew the cadmium plated screw which secures the
   casing to the bottom of the boot (trunk).
2. Disconnect the two ducts which connect the casing to the nozzles.

### SOFICA heater (first type)

<!-- PDF p.443 · N-8 -->

On the first type SOFICA heater the water feed to the radiator came through two hoses
connected to the radiator hoses. The thermostat was fitted in the upper hose.

**Thermostat:** CL 26 — Reference: 760 or 727 or 765.

**Specifications:**

| Item | Value |
| ---- | ----- |
| Starts to open | 73°C (163°F) |
| Fully opened | minimum of 6.5 mm (1/4") at a temperature of 83°C (181°F) |

## III — SOFICA heater — intense cold model (6 V, 12 V)

### Description (intense cold)

<!-- PDF p.444 · N-9 -->

![SOFICA "intense cold" heater — installation in the body and the front air-distribution control detail — PDF p.444](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0444-intense-cold-installation.webp)

The "INTENSE COLD" heating system comprises a two speed large output heater and two flexible
distribution ducts. Heating and demisting are by means of hot air supplied by the SOFICA
heater. The installation consists of:

- A Sofica heater in the engine compartment (on the right hand side).
- A control casing under the scuttle.
- An air outlet trap placed under the rear seat.
- Two flexible ducts connecting: the heater to the control casing, the heater to the rear
  trap.
- Two demisting nozzles.
- Two flexible ducts connecting the control casing to the demisting nozzles.
- An adjustable radiator blind (except for U.S.A. and Canada versions).

### Principle of operation (intense cold)

<!-- PDF p.445 · N-10 -->

![SOFICA "intense cold" heater — cutaway (nozzle 1, radiator 2, filter screen 3, fan 4, ducts 5) and the front air-distribution rods (A) operating flaps (B) — PDF p.445](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0445-intense-cold-principle.webp)

The SOFICA heater comprises:

- A nozzle (1) which carries air from the aperture in the radiator cardboard.
- A radiator (2) which heats the air.
- A removeable filter screen (3).
- A fan (4) which sucks warm air to force it into the two ducts (5).

The fan is controlled by means of a three position switch placed on the dashboard support.
It sucks air through the radiator and forces it into the flexible ducts which carry it
towards the control casing and the rear trap.

The front air distribution control (heating and demisting) is by means of two rods (A) which
operate flaps (B). At the rear the air comes up from the trap under the rear seat.

### Thermostat — specifications and checking (intense cold)

<!-- PDF p.446 · N-11 -->

The air is heated as it passes through the heater radiator through which the engine cooling
water is flowing. So that the heater comes quickly into operation there is a thermostat
fitted in the engine cooling system between the cylinder head and the engine cooling
radiator. This thermostat prevents the water being cooled by the engine radiator until it
has reached the temperature necessary to operate the thermostat, that is to say **82°C
(180°F)**. The water then flows through the small SOFICA heater radiator (following the
course shown by the arrows). As the quantity of the water is thus reduced it heats up more
quickly and brings the heater into operation sooner.

**Thermostat:** CL 26 — Reference 794 stamped on the inner flange.

**Specifications:**

| Item | Value |
| ---- | ----- |
| Starts to open | 82°C (180°F) |
| Fully opened | minimum of 6.5 mm (1/4") at a temperature of 93°C (200°F) |

**Checking:**

1. Place the thermostat in a container in which there is cold water, with its valve
   uppermost.
2. Heat the water whilst keeping it moving.
3. When the water temperature reaches **82°C (180°F)** the valve should start to open.
4. At **93°C (200°F)** the valve should be **6.5 mm (1/4")** open, at least.

> If it does not open at the correct time and by the correct amount, replace the thermostat.

> **NOTE:** When refitting it is essential to place the thermostatic spring on the cylinder
> head side.

### Removing and refitting the SOFICA heater (intense cold)

<!-- PDF p.447 · N-12 -->

![SOFICA "intense cold" heater removal, and the motor-fan unit exploded (four securing bolts A, rubber spacers B) — PDF p.447](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0447-intense-cold-removal.webp)

**Removing:**

1. Drain the engine water.
2. Disconnect the current input lead from the relay terminal.
3. Loosen the water hose clips and the air duct clips inside the car.
4. Disconnect the hoses and ducts at the heater.
5. Unscrew and remove the three nuts which secure the heater bracket.
6. Remove the heater.

**Refitting:**

1. Carry out the removing operations in reverse.
2. Fill the cooling system with water.
3. Bleed the heater system by unscrewing the knurled nut which is in the top right hand
   corner of the heater radiator.
4. Screw in the bleed screw. Top-up after running the engine for a few minutes.

### Dismantling and reassembling the "motor-fan" unit

<!-- PDF p.447 · N-12 -->

**Dismantling:**

1. Disconnect the earth (ground) lead from the bottom of the heater bracket.
2. Unscrew and remove the four bolts (A) which secure the motor fan unit to the bracket (two
   on each side).

> **NOTE:** Pay attention with the rubber spacers (B) placed between the bracket and the
> motor fan unit.

<!-- PDF p.448 · N-13 -->

![Motor-fan unit fully exploded — half casings (C, D), impeller securing nut (E, left-hand thread), impeller (F), snail housing (G), motor retaining nuts (H), motor (J), rubber washers (K), spacers (L) — PDF p.448](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0448-motor-fan-exploded.webp)

3. Disconnect the two half casings (C and D) and separate them.
4. Unscrew and remove nut (E) which secures impeller (F) to the motor shaft.

   > **WARNING:** Nut (E) has a left hand thread.

5. Remove the impeller (F) and snail housing (G).
6. Unscrew and remove the three nuts (H) which retain the motor (J) to the half housing.
   Remove the motor. Put aside the six rubber washers (K) and the two spacers (L).

**Reassembling:** Carry out the removing operations in reverse.

### Maintenance (intense cold)

<!-- PDF p.449 · N-14 -->

Every **5,000 km (3,000 miles)**, remove the filter screen, clean it with petrol
(gasoline), dry it with compressed air and spray lubri-filtre compound on it.

### Electrical circuit

<!-- PDF p.449 · N-14 -->

![SOFICA "intense cold" heater electrical circuit — 3-position switch (A/B/C), resistance (1) and motor (2) — PDF p.449](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0449-electrical-circuit.webp)

**Description.** The electrical system consists of:

- a) A lead which feeds current into the switch (+ lead).
- b) A switch with 3 positions: A, B and C.
- c) A resistance (1).
- d) A set of leads consisting of: 2 leads from the switch terminals to the resistance
  terminals, one lead connecting the resistance output terminal to the heater motor.
- e) An electric motor (2) which drives the fan.

**Principle of operation:**

| Switch position | Resistance (1) | Motor (2) state |
| --------------- | -------------- | --------------- |
| B | — | Not fed with current — "off" position |
| A | Short-circuited | Runs at high speed |
| C | Included in the circuit | Runs at a slower speed |
