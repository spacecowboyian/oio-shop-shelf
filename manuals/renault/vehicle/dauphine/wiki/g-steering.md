## II — Specifications

<!-- PDF p.271 · G-4 -->

Rack type steering with internal return spring contained in a sealed housing.

| Item | Value |
| ---- | ----- |
| Overall length of rack | 706 mm (27 13/16") |
| Overall length of rack (later, reduced) | 700 mm (27 9/16") |
| Reduction ratio | 24 to 1 |
| Turning circle diameter | 4.55 m (14 ft. 11 in.) |
| Steering wheel diameter | 400 mm (15 3/4") |

The length of the rack was later reduced to **700 mm (27 9/16")** to permit more clearance for
adjusting the front wheel alignment. This new rack can be fitted in place of the old **706 mm
(27 13/16")** rack.

## V — Determining the steering centre point and centralizing the steering wheel

<!-- PDF p.272 · G-5 -->

The steering wheel spokes must be horizontal when the front wheels are in the straight ahead position.

In this position, with the wheels straight ahead and the spokes horizontal, the steering wheel should
be in the centre of the travel through which it can be moved to right and left before starting to
compress the spring.

To do this, place the steering wheel on its splined shaft so that it can be moved through two equal
angles to right and left before connecting up the steering links. These angles are taken from the
horizontal position up to the point where resistance is felt owing to the spring being compressed.

## VI — Removing and refitting the steering mechanism

<!-- PDF p.272 · G-5 -->

**Removing:**

1. Remove the spare wheel and the front wheels.
2. Disconnect the steering links from the stub axle steering arms by means of extractor T.Av.05A.

> **NOTE:** One must tear off the protective cup before fitting tool T.Av.05A.

<!-- PDF p.273 · G-6 -->

3. Remove the two bolts (2) which connect the steering flexible coupling to the flange on the steering
   column side.
4. Unpin and remove the two bolts (1) which secure the steering mechanism to the brackets on the front
   cross member.
5. Remove the steering mechanism assembly through one side of the vehicle.

**Refitting:**

Carry out the removing operations in reverse.

1. Fit the bolts the correct way round to the flange on the steering column side (the bolt heads are
   to be on the steering box side, with plain washers under their heads).
2. Correctly align the steering wheel.
3. Tighten the nuts which secure the steering box to its brackets to a torque of **3.5 ± 0.5 m.kg
   (20 to 30 lbs.ft)** and split-pin the nuts.
4. Tighten and split-pin the nuts which secure the steering link ball joints.
5. Adjust the front wheel alignment and toe-in (see [Front Axle Chapter](h-front-axle.md)).

## VII — Dismantling, overhauling and reassembling the steering mechanism

<!-- PDF p.274 · G-7 -->

**Removing:**

See section VI.

**Overhauling:**

Mount the steering box on support Dir.20, which is to be gripped in a vice.

**Dismantling:**

1. Unlock and loosen the nuts; unscrew the steering links (1).
2. Remove the dust covers (2) and the attachment collars (3).
3. Remove the plunger circlip (4), the thrust washer (5), the spring (6) and the plunger (7).
   (Use tool Dir.19.)

<!-- PDF p.275 · G-8 -->

4. Remove the two flexible coupling securing bolts.
5. Remove the flexible coupling and its two plates.
6. Remove the flange set bolt.
7. Remove the flange (9), the upper circlip (10), the safety washer (11) and the spacer (12).

![Steering pinion and drive flange — exploded view (flange 9, upper circlip 10, safety washer 11, spacer 12, seal 13, circlip 14, thrust washer 15, grease nipple 16, bearing 17, pinion 18, sealing washer 19, circlip 20) — PDF p.275](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0275-pinion-flange-exploded.webp)

8. Remove seal (13) by means of extractor Dir.16.
9. Remove circlip (14), thrust washer (15) — by centralizing it — and grease nipple (16).
10. Knock out the pinion (18) and bearing (17).
11. Remove the sealing washer (19) and the circlip (20) from behind bearing (17).

<!-- PDF p.276 · G-9 -->

12. Separate bearing (17) from the pinion (18) on the press.
13. Remove the steering box cover and its paper gasket.
14. Remove snap rings (21) from cups (22) in the following manner:
    - **a) For the snap ring at the opposite end to the rack teeth:** Rest a screwdriver against the
      steering box housing and lift and free one end of the snap ring, then, using a **17 mm**
      open-ended spanner (wrench) across the flats of the rack (at the toothed end), turn the rack so
      that the snap ring is freed from its groove.
    - **b) For the snap ring at the rack teeth end:** Pull the rack towards you from the toothed end
      and the snap ring at this end will be removed from its location when it makes contact with the
      steering box bush.
15. Free the return spring (24), the cups (22) and the rubber sleeve (25).

> **NOTE:** The snap rings, circlips and seals are not to be re-used.

<!-- PDF p.277 · G-10 -->

16. Push the lower bearing bush (26) of pinion (18) inwards.

### Replacing the bearing bushes

<!-- PDF p.277 · G-10 -->

Use the repair kit consisting of bushes and snap rings to replace the bearing bushes.

1. Extract the two bearing bushes by means of extracting mandrel Dir.17.
2. Push in bush (27) on the pinion side on the press (with the lead chamfer facing inwards).
3. Fit the new circlip, ensuring that it is a tight fit in its groove.

> **NOTE:** If it is not necessary to replace the bearing bushes, the bush at the opposite end to the
> steering pinion is still to be removed in order to make reassembling easier.

**Reassembling:**

<!-- PDF p.278 · G-11 -->

![Steering rack reassembly — sectional view showing snap rings (21), return spring (24), rubber sleeve (25), cups (22), lower bearing bush (26) and rack bearing bush (27), with the Dir.18C insertion end fitting and snap-ring retaining fork — PDF p.278](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0278-rack-reassembly-section.webp)

1. Fit the lower bearing bush (26) from inside the housing.
2. Grease the unit and place a reserve of grease in that part of the housing behind the rack bearing
   bush (27).
3. Insert rubber sleeve (25) into spring (24).
4. Compress the spring together with its 2 cups (22) and fit it in place.
5. Fit a new snap ring (21) to the rack in the groove at the smooth end. (Ensure that it is a tight
   fit in its groove.)
6. Insert rack (23) together with its insertion end fitting from tooling Dir.18C from the toothed side
   until the end fitting reaches approximately the centre of the spring, then slide it through the
   rubber sleeve (25).
7. Insert the second new snap ring (21) onto the inserting end fitting.
8. Place the snap ring retaining fork from tooling Dir.18C against the housing.

<!-- PDF p.279 · G-12 -->

9. Slide the rack until the snap ring (21) enters its groove. Remove the end fitting and the fork.
10. Insert bearing bush (27) at the opposite end to the steering pinion by means of a tube and fit the
    new circlip.
11. Refit the steering box housing cover with a new paper gasket and fit the mounting lug. Before
    these operations, fill the housing with "carter F" grease.
12. Fit a new circlip (20) to the pinion.
13. Fit bearing (17) to the pinion (18).

> In the case of the arrangement which has no nylon cage (Fig. 1 — paragraph IV): no special
> precaution. In the case of the old type arrangement (Fig. 2 — paragraph IV), the full face of the
> nylon cage is to be towards the rack.

<!-- PDF p.280 · G-13 -->

14. Fit a new sealing washer (19) into the groove in the pinion.
15. Position the smooth portion on the serrated end fitting of pinion (18) opposite the pressure
    spring.
16. Fit the following in the order stated below: thrust washer (15), a new circlip (14), a new sealing
    ring (13) which has been previously oiled, spacer (12), safety washer (11) and a new upper circlip
    (10).
17. Position the drive flange without tightening its set bolt, so that the centre-line passing through
    the two holes is square with the steering wheel centreline.
18. Fit hand press (Dir.19). Place the two studs on the end of the tool screw head in the two holes in
    the flange, and the spigot on the press body in the grease nipple location.

<!-- PDF p.281 · G-14 -->

19. Fit:
    - Plunger (17). <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: source misprint, not OCR — page image clearly prints "Plunger (17)"; the plunger is part (7) per the G-7 dismantling list (plunger circlip 4, thrust washer 5, spring 6, plunger 7). "(17)" is the pinion bearing elsewhere; printed "17" implies "7". -->
    - Spring (6).
    - Thrust washer (5).
    - A new circlip (4).
20. Compress this assembly by means of screw (C) on hand press (Dir.19).
21. Insert circlip (4) into its groove by means of a pair of internal circlip pliers.
22. Loosen screw (C).

### Adjusting the steering pinion end play

<!-- PDF p.281 · G-14 -->

![Adjusting the steering pinion end play — plunger assembly (parts 4–7) and the Dir.19 hand-press pre-load setup, screw (C), thrust washer (5), lever flexible arm (D), screw head (E) and flange set bolt (B) — PDF p.281](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0281-pinion-endplay-adjustment.webp)

This is the only method to be used:

1. Pull up screw (C) against thrust washer (5).
2. Tighten the drive flange until the flexible arm (D) on the lever makes contact with the screw head
   (E).
3. Tighten the flange set bolt (B). Tightening torque **2.25 ± 0.25 m.kg (15 lbs.ft)**.
   <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: OCR read "2.25 m.ket 0.25 m.ke (15 Lbs ft"; page image clearly shows "2.25 m.kg ± 0.25 m.kg (15 lb ft)" — corrected from image. -->

<!-- PDF p.282 · G-15 -->

> Under no circumstances must the flexible coupling be fitted to the flange when the pre-load is
> adjusted or when the rack is fitted.

4. Remove the hand press.

> **NOTE:** Lever (D) on hand press (Dir.19) is **8 mm (5/16")** in diameter and this produces a
> pre-load, when tightening screw (E), of approximately **150 kg (330 lbs)**.

5. Screw grease nipple (16) into the lower bearing (26), ensuring that you position it correctly
   (**45° towards the wheel**).
6. Position the flexible coupling, fitted with its two sheet steel washers, to the flange.
7. Insert the two securing bolts with flat washers under their heads (nuts on the steering box side).
8. Screw up the two bolts alternately whilst holding the two nuts on the steering box side with an
   open-ended spanner (wrench).
9. Tighten and split-pin.
10. Fit the dust covers (2) to the steering mechanism with attachment collars (3).
11. Screw the steering links into the rack. Do not tighten the nuts, to permit adjustment of the
    toe-in after the steering has been refitted to the vehicle.

> **NOTE:** The steering links are handed. In the operating position on the vehicle the grease nipple
> on the ball joint at the end of the link should face towards the rear.

## VIII — Overhauling the steering links

<!-- PDF p.283 · G-16 -->

![Steering link — exploded view: rack end (part A, link end fitting 28) and stub axle end (part B, grease nipple 1) — PDF p.283](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0283-steering-links-exploded.webp)

**Dismantling**

**Rack end, part A:**

1. Unpin the link end fitting (28) and unscrew it.
2. Remove the cup, the spring and the locking washer. Unscrew the nut and separate the ball joint from
   the end fitting (28).

**Stub axle end, part B:**

1. Remove the circlip and take out the spacer plug, the spring, the upper cup, the ball joint and the
   lower cup.

> **NOTE:** It is forbidden to fit spacing washers in end fitting (28) or to grind back this end
> fitting at the serrated end. Such modifications would jam the ball joint and cause its shank to
> break.

Only the position of the grease nipple (1) identifies a right-hand steering link from a left hand one.
The picture shows a right hand link (as seen from the driving position).

**Reassembling**

**Rack end, part A:**

1. Insert the ball joint, smeared with graphite grease, into end fitting (28).
2. Fit a new dust cover.
3. Screw up the nut and fit a new locking washer.
4. Place the cup and the spring in end fitting (28).
5. Screw end fitting (28) fully in and pin it.

**Stub axle end, part B:**

1. Fit the lower cup, the ball joint (smeared with graphite grease), the upper cup, the spring and the
   spacing plug.
2. Compress the whole assembly and fit a new circlip. Fit a new dust cover.
3. Screw in the grease nipples and correctly position them.

## IX — Removing and refitting the steering column

<!-- PDF p.284 · G-17 -->

![Steering column — exploded view: flange (9), flexible coupling (2), steering lock (4), column (5), bracket (7), rubber mat (12), felt (13), insulating plate (14), sealing washer/bush (15), nut (16), steering wheel (17) — PDF p.284](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0284-steering-column-exploded.webp)

**Removing:**

1. Place the car on stands.
2. Remove the spare wheel.
3. Disconnect the battery.
4. Unscrew the nuts (1) which secure the steering column flange (9) to the flexible coupling (2).
5. Place the steering lock (4) in the "garage" position.
6. Remove the nut (16).
7. Remove the steering wheel (17) by means of tool (Dir.21).
8. Disconnect all the electrical leads from the horn and lighting switch and the anti-theft cartridge,
   marking them, after having removed the horn and lighting switch casing (see
   [Electrical Equipment](d-electrical-and-ignition.md)).
9. Remove the nuts (6) which secure bracket (7).
10. Slide the bracket (7) upwards to free the steering column (5) and the wiring.
11. Push back the rubber mat (12) and the felt (13). Take out the insulating plate (14) after removing
    the two screws which secure the assembly.
12. Push out sealing washer (15) from its location.
13. Take out the steering column (5) from inside the vehicle (a hole is provided in the floor to allow
    flange (9) to pass through).

**Refitting:**

Carry out the removing operations in reverse, taking the following precautions:

- The nuts (6) which secure bracket (7) are only to be tightened after the operation is complete.
- Tighten castellated nuts (1) which secure the steering flange (9) to the flexible coupling. Torque:
  **0.500 ± 0.100 m.kg (5 lbs.ft)**, and split-pin them.
  <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: source conversion, not OCR — page image confirms "0.500 ± 0.100 m.kg (5 lbs.ft)". 0.5 m.kg ≈ 3.6 lbf·ft, so the printed "5 lbs.ft" is a loose/rounded imperial figure; the metric 0.500 m.kg is authoritative. -->

- Check that all the horn and lighting switch services operate normally. (Check each position.)
- Grease bush (15) with Spagraph grease.
- When refitting the steering wheel, align it.

## XI — Replacing the steering wheel

<!-- PDF p.285 · G-18 -->

1. Remove the embellisher.
2. Unscrew the steering wheel securing nut.
3. Remove the steering wheel using extractor Dir.21.

When refitting, the wheels should be in the "straight ahead" position and the spokes of the steering
wheel should form equal angles with respect to the horizontal (see paragraph V).

**Steering wheel securing nut tightening torque: 5.5 m.kg (40 lbs.ft).**

## XII — Removing and refitting the steering lock

<!-- PDF p.286 · G-19 -->

![Steering lock (anti-theft cartridge) removal — locking tab (1), securing screws (2), bracket (7) — PDF p.286](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0286-steering-lock.webp)

**Removing:**

1. Disconnect the battery.
2. Remove the two screws from the horn and lighting switch casing and take off the right hand portion.
3. Place the steering lock switch in the "garage" position.
4. Take out the key.
5. Disconnect the three leads, marking them.
6. Remove the two screws (2) which secure the anti-theft cartridge to bracket (7).
7. Press on the locking ball with a scriber point.
8. Remove the locking system whilst depressing the tab (1).

**Refitting:**

1. Place the locking device in the "garage" position.
2. Carry out the removing operations in reverse.
