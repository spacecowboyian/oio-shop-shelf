Chapter C of the M.R.93 workshop manual. It covers the clutch in its four forms fitted to the Dauphine
range: the conventional cable-operated single dry-disc clutch, and three versions of the "Ferlec"
electromagnetic (automatic) clutch — the standard 6-volt Ferlec and the simplified 6-volt (Type 510-03)
and 12-volt (Type 510-05) Ferlec systems.

## Conventional clutch

<!-- PDF p.70 · C-3 · variant contents page -->

Single dry-disc clutch, cable-operated by the pedal through a withdrawal lever, fork and graphite
withdrawal pad. Contents: Identification, Specifications, Principles of operation, Replacing the clutch
withdrawal pad, Replacing the clutch withdrawal fork, Adjusting the clutch clearance.

### Identification

<!-- PDF p.71 · C-4 -->

The type number is stamped on the front face of the cover.

- Type **PKH 4/5** and **PKH 5** — for the R.1090 and the R.1091.
- Type **PKH 5/2** — for the R.1093.

> **Note:** Type PKH 4/5 and PKH 5 clutches can be replaced by the type PKH 4/8 clutch at present fitted in
> production. When doing this, one must replace the clutch friction disc. Only type PKH 4/8 is at present
> supplied by the C.S.S.

### Specifications

Single disc clutch operating dry, make FERODO. Friction disc with elastic hub, make FERODO. Graphite
withdrawal pad.

| Item                                    | Value                            |
| --------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Thickness of disc — type PKH 4/5, PKH 5 | 7 mm (.276")                     |
| Thickness of disc — type PKH 5/2        | 7.4 mm (.292")                   |
| Clutch clearance (at lever or fork)     | 2 to 3 mm (5/64" to 1/8")        |

Clutch pressure plate carrying rated springs:

| Type    | Number of springs | Colour                 |
| ------- | ----------------- | ---------------------- |
| PKH 4/5 | 6                 | Black                  |
| PKH 5   | 9                 | Pink                   |
| PKH 5/2 | 6                 | White (3), Green (3)   |

### Principles of operation

<!-- PDF p.72 · C-5 -->

The pedal movement is transmitted by a cable to the clutch withdrawal lever, which is secured to the fork
by means of the shaft (2). The withdrawal fork (1) pivots and moves the withdrawal pad (3) and the ring (4)
forwards. This ring makes contact with the three toggle levers (5).

The toggle levers swing, and as they are secured to plate (6) by means of bolt (7), cause the plate (6) to
move back and compress the springs (8). At this point the friction disc (9) is no longer clamped between
the flywheel (10) and the plate (6), and the drive between the engine and gear box (transmission case) is
thus disconnected.

![Conventional clutch — cross-section showing operation (1 withdrawal fork, 2 shaft, 3 withdrawal pad, 4 ring, 5 toggle levers, 6 pressure plate, 7 bolt, 8 springs, 9 friction disc, 10 flywheel) — PDF p.72](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0072-conventional-clutch-section.webp)

### Replacing the clutch withdrawal pad

Place the "carrier – pad" assembly on the clutch withdrawal fork with the set bolt on the opposite side to
the fork. Clip the two retaining springs (1) into position (their straight ends in the holes in the centres
of the pad carrier trunions and their hooked ends in the pad holes).

### Replacing the clutch withdrawal fork

<!-- PDF p.73 · C-6 -->

1. Place the following on the shaft: the return spring (1), with its bent end on the lever side, and rubber
   washer (2).
2. Insert the shaft into the first bearing followed by spacer (3) and fork (4) — with the groove into which
   the withdrawal pad fits on the opposite side to the gear box.
3. Secure the fork to the shaft (pinhead uppermost).
4. Refit the clutch withdrawal pad.

![Clutch withdrawal fork — exploded view (1 return spring, 2 rubber washer, 3 spacer, 4 fork, 5 pad carrier) — PDF p.73](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0073-withdrawal-fork-exploded.webp)

### Adjusting the clutch clearance

Loosen lock nut (3) at the end of the cable (near the lever) and screw the nut (2) in or out to obtain the
clearance at the lever equal to **A = 2 to 3 mm (5/64" to 1/8")**.

### Clutch operating principles (standard Ferlec 6 V)

<!-- PDF p.76 · C-10 -->

The coil housing (1) and intermediate plate (6) assembly and the armature plate (5) and pressure plate (13)
assembly are connected mechanically by drive strips (4). In this way, the rotational movement of the first
assembly drives the second.

The clutch is disengaged by spring (16) when there is no current flow. When coil (3) is energised it
attracts the armature plate (5) towards the coil housing and the pressure plate (13) grips the friction
disc against the intermediate plate (6): the clutch engages. Strips (15) take up the plate in the
transmission.

### Adjusting the clutch air gap (standard Ferlec 6 V)

Connect a lead between the terminal of the lead from the rheostat brushes (rheostat case) and the "battery"
terminal of the voltage regulator (15). Place the two-way switch (6) in the dynamo position (a). The clutch
is energised.

At three points, 120° apart, there should be the same clearance (J) between the coil housing and the
armature plate, which should be between **0.20 and 0.41 mm (.008 – .016")**.

If the gap is smaller than this, remove the three adjusting shims (1) from between the armature plate and
the pressure plate. Return the adjusting resistance to its correct setting.

### Removing the friction disc (standard Ferlec 6 V)

Remove the power unit assembly after first disconnecting the "Ferlec" clutch feed leads. Remove the carbon
brushes and the carbon brush carriers. Disconnect the feed shunt from the coil housing. Remove the pressure
plate and put aside the adjusting shims if there are any. For those clutches which have manufacturing serial
numbers smaller than 213 – 55, mark the positions of the shims. Remove the friction disc.

> **NOTE:** The clutch manufacturing number is on the front face of the coil housing.

### Checking the friction disc (standard Ferlec 6 V)

Check:

- the elasticity of the play take-up strips.
- that the friction disc segments are of the same thickness. Thickness tolerance ± 0.04 mm (.002").
- the buckle (mandrel Emb.06 and a dial indicator). Maximum permissible 0.1 mm (.004").
- surface finish (see cleaning mechanism).

### Removing the buckle from the friction plate (standard Ferlec 6 V)

If the buckle is near the tolerance of ± 0.1 mm (.004") use fork Emb.08 to return it to within the
tolerance. If the distortion is too great, replace the friction disc.

### Checking the mechanism (standard Ferlec 6 V)

**1. Checking the intermediate plate.**

a) Checking for buckle. The buckle should not exceed 0.04 mm (.002"). If the buckle is greater than
0.04 mm (.002") check the crankshaft locating faces (clean them) or replace the mechanism.

b) Checking for bow. The maximum clearance between the straight edge and the plate should be less than
0.04 mm (.002"). If the clearance is greater than this, replace the mechanism. This check can be carried out
without removing the mechanism if a sufficiently short straight edge is used.

**2. Checking the pressure plate.** Checking for bow: the maximum clearance between the straight edge and
the plate should be less than 0.04 mm (.002"). If the clearance is greater than this, replace the mechanism.

**3. Checking the current consumption** of the clutch coil in the coil housing: it should be between
**5 – 6 amps at a voltage of 6 volts**.

### Removing the clutch mechanism (standard Ferlec 6 V)

<!-- PDF p.79 · C-12 -->

Remove the friction disc (see [Removing the friction disc](#removing-the-friction-disc-standard-ferlec-6-v)).
Mark the position of the clutch mechanism with respect to the crankshaft, and remove it. (There are four
connecting bolts.)

### Dismantling and reassembling the clutch mechanism (standard Ferlec 6 V)

**Overhauling.** The operations that can be carried out on the mechanism are as follows:

- Dismantling the pressure plate when the friction disc is damaged (see removing the friction disc).
- Dismantling the intermediate plate, only in the case of breakage of a drive strip (see the "FERODO"
  instructions which accompany the kits of spare parts).
- Cleaning the coil housing – armature plate – intermediate plate assembly in trichlorethylene (dry them
  with a compressed air gun).
- Cleaning the slip rings with alcohol and the friction faces and friction disc with trichlorethylene.

### Refitting the mechanism (standard Ferlec 6 V)

1. Clean the locating faces on the mechanism and on the crankshaft.
2. Refit the mechanism in the position marked during removing, with respect to the crankshaft.
3. Tighten and lock the four bolts.
4. Fit the friction disc, with the strips towards the outside, followed by the pressure plate.
5. Fit the adjusting shims if there are any and connect and varnish the shunt.
6. Refit the brush carriers and the carbon brushes.
7. Fit locating mandrel Emb.02.
8. Energise the clutch, remove the mandrel and connect the engine to the gear box (transmission case).

### Sealed "SERMEC" microswitch

<!-- PDF p.80 · C-13 -->

**Function and principles of operation.** The microswitch is a two-position switch which feeds the clutch
from the dynamo through the first-reverse, 2nd–3rd rheostat, depending upon the position of the gear change
lever by which it is operated.

**Adjusting.** Fit the bracket so that whichever gear is engaged contact is still made despite the vibration
movement of the gear change lever. It cannot be reconditioned.

### Checking the microswitch on the vehicle

1. Disconnect leads 1 and 7 from the rheostat case.
2. Connect a pilot light between lead 1 and earth (ground).
3. Place the gear change lever in the first-reverse position.
4. Accelerate gently. The pilot light will switch on if the microswitch is correctly positioned and in
   working order.
5. Check in the same way for the 2nd–3rd position, connecting the pilot light between lead 7 and earth
   (ground).

### Checking the relay

Place the rheostat in the maximum resistance position. When the relay is energised, by closing the switch,

switch off.

### Adjusting the "Ferlec" rheostat case linkage

<!-- PDF p.82 · C-15 -->

1. Disconnect one of the two ball joint casings.
2. Place the rheostat lever positioning tool Emb.11 on the case with its rod entering the hole in the lever.
3. Adjust the length of the link.
4. Refit the ball joint and remove the tool.

> **NOTE:** This adjustment can be carried out by any repair shop which possesses tool Emb.11.

### Function and principles of operation of the "GEMA" two-way switch

This is mounted in the instrument panel and provides a manual control which selects either feed from the
dynamo or feed from the battery to the clutch. It also cuts off both feeds. This switch cannot be repaired.

### "Ferlec" gear change lever

<!-- PDF p.83 · C-16 -->

**Function and principles of operation.** This selects gear changes and energises the relay in the rheostat
case, thus disengaging the clutch, when operated towards the front or towards the rear.

**Checking the gear change lever.** Connect a pilot light and a battery between the connection clip and
earth (ground). Operate the lever turning it 1/5 of a turn after each operation. The light should switch on
in each of the five positions.

**Adjusting the contact spring.** On levers of the latest type there is no longer a groove on stud (7) to
position the contact spring (4). Fit it so that A = a + b = 25.4 −0/−0.5 mm (63/64 to 1"). Measure a + b
separately. The new contact spring has 11 coils in place of the former 8 coils.

**Adjusting the sleeve.** The sleeve (5) is oval in shape at its upper end. The largest possible movement
should be towards the front and rear. When refitting, screw the sleeve fully in, then unscrew it to obtain
this position. Following this, tighten lock nut (6).

**Repairing.** This is limited to replacing the silver plated contact spring (4) and the earth lead. For
fitting the earth lead, see the Ferodo instructions supplied with the set of spare parts.

> **IMPORTANT NOTE:** When replacing the gear change lever, it is essential that the fork end pin split pin
> should be fitted on the opposite side to the curved slack portion in the lead.

## Simplified "Ferlec" clutch — 6 Volt (Type 510-03)

### Principles of operation (simplified Ferlec 6 V)

<!-- PDF p.88 · C-22 -->

**Type 510-03. Operating voltage: 6 volts.** The simplified "Ferlec" clutch was introduced in three stages:

- **1st stage:** Comprised fitting a simplified rheostat case and the corresponding wiring harness. This was
  from "Dauphine" No. 279 508.
- **2nd stage:** The fitting of new brush carriers: from No. 323 693 engine type 670-1.
- **3rd stage:** The fitting of simplified clutch mechanism from No. 358 524 engine type 670-1.

#### Clutch mechanism construction (simplified Ferlec 6 V)

Type BHA 5 clutch mechanism. <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: OCR read the type as "BHA 5"; on the page image the leading letter is an ambiguous typewriter glyph (reads as "EHA" or "BHA"). The 12-Volt parallel (C-41) has the same ambiguity ("BHA 8"/"EHA 8"). Kept the OCR reading; verify the leading letter against a cleaner scan. -->
The mechanism consists of two sub-assemblies:

a) The intermediate plate (4) which is secured to the coil housing (1) at three points. This sub-assembly is
secured to the crankshaft.

b) The armature plate (3) which is secured to the pressure plate (6) at three points.

![Simplified "Ferlec" 6-Volt clutch (Type 510-03) — exploded view (parts 1-12) and cross-section — PDF p.88](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0088-simplified-6v-clutch-exploded.webp)

<!-- PDF p.89 · C-23 -->

The friction disc (5) is between the intermediate plate (4) and the pressure plate (6). The first "coil
housing – intermediate plate" sub-assembly, in turning, drives the second through the three strips (2). The
endwise movement of the second "armature plate – pressure plate" assembly grips the friction disc between
the intermediate plate (4) which is stationary, and the pressure plate (6). The coil (11) and the coil
housing (1) are fed with current by two slip rings (8) and (9), one of which is connected up by means of
strip (7), the other being earthed (grounded).

Current flowing through the coil causes magnetic attraction and under this the moving part, "the armature
plate – pressure plate assembly", moves nearer to the coil housing and thus grips the friction disc. When
the current is switched off the three strips (10), on studs (12), push the "armature plate – pressure plate"
moving assembly outwards and thus release the friction disc.

**Operating voltage: 6 volts.**

| No. | Component                                       |
| --- | ----------------------------------------------- |

| 2   | Gear change lever                               |
| 3   | Microswitch under floor                         |

| 5   | Ignition coil                                   |

<!-- PDF p.90 · C-24 -->

**Special features.** As the circuit is common, the clutch can be disengaged by the gear change lever, no
matter what the feed selected (dynamo or battery). When the battery feeds the system, progressivity is
provided by the rheostat.

| Coil          | Value                        |
| ------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Resistance    | 0.98 +0.05/−0.04 ohms        |
| Current       | 5.82 to 6.37 A               |

**Rheostat progressivity.**

*1st-reverse position — microswitch open.* The rheostat resistance decreases from stud A (idling speed) to
stud D (accelerated) by the successive elimination of resistances R1 + R2, R3 and R4.

*2nd – 3rd position — microswitch closed:*
- Carbon brush on stud A (idling speed): resistances R4 + R1 = 0.25 + 0.95 = 1.20 ohms are in circuit; the
  microswitch (3) short circuits resistances R2 + R3.
- Carbon brush on stud B: the two resistances R2 + R3 are connected in parallel by the closing of
  microswitch (3), and their equivalent resistance is R' = (R2 × R3)/(R2 + R3) = approx. 0.28 ohms. The sum
  of the resistances in circuit at this stud is therefore R4 + R' = 0.25 + 0.28 = 0.53 ohms.
- Carbon brush on stud C: only resistance R4, which equals 0.25 ohms, remains in circuit.
- Carbon brush on stud D: there is no resistance in circuit.

Resistances in circuit:

| Cursor / carbon brush on stud | First-reverse — resistance in circuit | 2nd – 3rd — resistance in circuit |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| A                             | R4 + R3 + R2 + R1 = 2.5 ohms          | R4 + R1 = 1.2 ohms                |
| B                             | R4 + R3 = 1.15 ohms                   | R4 + R' = 0.53 ohms               |
| C                             | R4 = 0.25 ohms                        | R4 = 0.25 ohms                    |
| D                             | Nil = 0 ohms                          | Nil = 0 ohms                      |

### Adjusting the rheostat case (simplified Ferlec 6 V)

<!-- PDF p.92 · C-26 -->

**Adjusting resistance.** Wear on the friction disc results in reduction of the air gap J and this means that
the clutch becomes less and less progressive in its operation. To return to the normal progressive operation
when the magnetic attraction takes place, one must reduce the strength of the current acting on the coil by
increasing the strength of the adjusting resistance (10). If this electrical adjustment is insufficient to
return the clutch to its former state, the air gap J must be returned to its normal figure. To increase the
strength of the adjusting resistance, move the resistance clip towards the right hand side of the vehicle.
This adjustment should be made by moving the clip by small amounts at a time, checking the result after each
movement by testing the car.

**Adjusting the control linkage.** To obtain progressive operation, the rheostat cursor must be in a very
definite position, when the engine is at idling speed. This position is obtained by using rheostat lever
positioning tool Emb.11 which is placed against the rheostat case, with its stud in a hole in the rheostat
lever. If this stud cannot be correctly fitted, adjust the rheostat control adjustable link. This adjustment
should only follow an adjustment of the engine idling speed.

**Adjusting the clutch mechanism air gap.** Place the switch under the dashboard in the "battery" position,
to feed the mechanism with current. Measure the air gap J at three points 120° apart between the armature
plate and the coil housing using a set of feeler gauges. This gap should be between **0.20 – 0.41 mm (.008"
to .016")**. If it is smaller than this, remove the three adjusting shims (13) which are between the armature
plate and the pressure plate. This operation is carried out through the apertures under the clutch housing.
The air gap adjustment should be followed by adjustment of the adjusting resistance.

### Microswitch (simplified Ferlec 6 V)

<!-- PDF p.93 · C-27 -->

**Function.** The microswitch is operated by the gear change link and it cuts out one part of the rheostat
case resistance when the gear change lever is in the 2nd–3rd position.

**Adjusting.**
1. Engage 3rd gear.
2. Push the gear change lever towards the left of the vehicle to take up the various clearances in the
   linkage.
3. Secure the microswitch so that the flexible strip just makes contact with the gear change lever clevis.
4. Engage first gear and push the gear change lever towards the right of the vehicle. Contact should be
   broken. Check this electrically.

**Checking the adjustment electrically.** Disconnect lead 5 from the case (yellow sleeve). Place instrument
panel switch in the "battery" position. Connect a pilot light between the disconnected lead 5 and earth
(ground) (voltage regulator securing point). Engage 1st or reverse — the light should switch off. Engage 2nd
or 3rd — the light should switch on. When the lever is engaged in a gear, check that the degree of movement
in the control bar, caused by the play in the hinges, does not alter the switch position (switching the
light on or off).

### Gear change lever (simplified Ferlec 6 V)

<!-- PDF p.94 · C-28 -->

**Function.** This earths (grounds) the rheostat case relay energising circuit, which cuts the current feed
to the mechanism. This then disengages.

**Principles of operation.** This earthing circuit is made by contact with the silver spring (14) (which is
earthed on the lever body) with the centre stud (15) through which the current enters the lever. This
contact is only made when the lever is moved forwards or backwards, that is to say in the direction in which
the gears are engaged, owing to the elongated hole at the top of the sleeve (16). The switch assembly
returns to its off position under the influence of spring (17).

**Adjusting the silver plated spring.** Screw the new silver plated spring (14) on to the lever stud (18) so
that A = 25.4 −0/−0.5 mm (63/64" – 1"). The silver plated spring has 11 coils.

**Adjusting the sleeve.** Sleeve (16) comprises an oval slot at the top. The centre line of this slot should
lie along the longitudinal centre line of the car. When refitting, screw the sleeve as far as it will go
then unscrew it by a maximum of half a turn to obtain the correct position. Then tighten lock nut (19).

**Reconditioning.** This is limited to replacing the silver plated spring (14) and the feed lead. When
fitting this last item, follow the Ferodo instructions which are supplied with the set of spare parts
(repair kit 8.541.098).

### Electrical check on the lever and on the relay (simplified Ferlec 6 V)

<!-- PDF p.95 · C-29 -->

**Electrical check on the lever.** Disconnect lead (1) (pink sleeve) and (2) (black sleeve) from the case.
Connect a pilot light between these two leads. Switch the dashboard switch to the "battery" position. Select
a gear. The light should switch on during the engagement of the gear and switch off as soon as the pressure
of the hand is removed from the lever.

**Electrical check on the relay.** Disconnect lead (6) from the case (violet sleeve). Connect a pilot light

Switch on the ignition switch. The light should switch on. It should switch off as soon as a gear is
engaged.

### Clutch mechanism (simplified Ferlec 6 V)

<!-- PDF p.96 · C-30 -->

**Removing the friction disc.** Remove the power unit assembly after having disconnected the "Ferlec" clutch
feed leads. Remove the carbon brushes and carbon brush carriers. Disconnect the feed strip (7) on the coil
housing. Remove the pressure plate (6) and put aside the air gap adjusting shims if there are any. Remove
the friction disc.

**Checking the friction disc.** Check:
- that the friction disc segments are of the same thickness. Tolerance ± 0.04 mm (.002").
- Thickness of new segments: 2.5 mm (.009") <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: source misprint, not OCR — the page image prints "(.009")", but 2.5 mm ≈ .098" and the 12-Volt parallel page (C-51) prints "2.5 mm (.099")". The imperial value here appears to have a dropped digit. Kept exactly as printed. -->

- Check the surface finish.

**Checking the pressure plate.** Check the plate for bow. The maximum clearance between the straight edge and
the plate should be less than 0.04 mm (.002"). If the clearance is greater than this, the disc can be refaced
(see NOTE under [Overhauling](#overhauling-the-mechanism-simplified-ferlec-6-v)). Check the surface finish; if
it is scored, reface it.

### Checking the intermediate plate (simplified Ferlec 6 V)

<!-- PDF p.97 · C-31 -->

**Check for buckle.** The buckle in the plate should not be greater than 0.04 mm (.002"). If the buckle is
greater than 0.04 mm (.002"), check the face along which the coil housing locates on the crankshaft (clean
it) or reface the intermediate plate (see NOTE).

**Checking for bow.** The maximum clearance between the straight edge and the plate must be less than
0.04 mm (.002"). If the clearance is greater than this, the plate may be refaced (see NOTE). This check can
be carried out without removing the clutch mechanism as long as the straight edge used is sufficiently
short. Check the surface finish; if it is scored, reface it.

> **NOTE:** It is possible to reface the intermediate plate and the pressure plate so that it may be re-used.
> Up to 0.2 mm (.008") may be removed. However, if the intermediate plate or the pressure plate are
> replaced, this involves replacing the entire clutch. The machining operations should be carefully carried
> out in order to remain within the dimensional and surface finish tolerances necessary for the clutch to
> operate correctly. One must machine the pressure plate securing lugs by an amount which is equal to the sum
> of the amounts removed from the pressure plate (6) and the intermediate plate (4). The four-bladed friction
> disc and the six-bladed friction disc are interchangeable. New slip rings can be skimmed to ensure that
> they are absolutely concentric with the mechanism and to remove any buckle. The crankshaft locating face on
> the flywheel can also be refaced if it is not perfectly flat.

### Overhauling the mechanism (simplified Ferlec 6 V)

<!-- PDF p.98 · C-32 -->

The coil housing, the armature plate, the intermediate plate and the pressure plate cannot be obtained
separately. If one of these components is damaged, the entire clutch mechanism is to be replaced. The
mechanism is supplied under the part No. 8.542.068.

Apart from the operations just described, the clutch mechanism overhauling operations are limited to:

**a) Cleaning the mechanism.** Too great a quantity of oil in the air gap may attract dust, the accumulation
of which will prevent the armature plate approaching the coil housing. Unlock the four bolts. Remove the
mechanism and immerse it for 5 minutes in a container of trichlorethylene (no other product is to be used).
It is to be dried with compressed air or by leaving it for half an hour in the open air.

**b) Replacing the drive strips.**
1. Mark the position of armature plate (3) with respect to coil housing (1).
2. Unlock and unscrew the three nuts from the elastic armature plate stop (20).
3. Remove the three bolts (21) which secure the strips (2) to the coil housing (1) and remove armature plate
   (3).
4. Free the drive strips from the armature plate. (Clean the parts with trichlorethylene.)
5. Fit the three new drive strips to the armature plate, ensuring that the parts are fitted in the correct
   order. Do not tighten the nuts (22) on the retaining bolts.

### Adjusting the mechanism before refitting (simplified Ferlec 6 V)

<!-- PDF p.99 · C-33 -->

Refit the mechanism assembly by following the "armature plate – coil housing" position marks made during
dismantling.

- If the original friction disc is refitted, insert a shim 0.2 mm (.008") thick under each lug on the
  pressure plate if these shims were in position when the mechanism was dismantled.
- If one is obliged to fit a new friction disc, insert a new shim 0.2 mm (.008") thick under each lug.

Check that the air gap of 0.20 mm – 0.41 mm (.008 to .016") is constant at three points around the clutch
using a set of feeler gauges and feed the clutch with 6 volts current.

Then continue reassembly:
1. Replace the three thrust washers (23) and place the armature plate against the coil housing (there is
   only one possible position).
2. Engage the ends of the drive strips into their locations on the coil housing and fit bolts (21) which
   connect them to the coil housing, without omitting the plain and shake-proof washers.
3. Tighten the three bolts (21) and the three nuts (22): **tightening torque 1 m.kg. (10 lbs/ft)**.

> **NOTE:** The repair kit for replacing the strips is to be ordered under the number 8.540.863.

**Adjust the armature plate elastic stops (12):**
1. Remove the friction disc and refit the pressure plate (with the 0.2 mm (.008") shims in position where
   applicable).
2. Insert a 2.90 mm (.114") spacer between the pressure plate (6) and the intermediate plate (4) near the
   armature plate stop and adjust it (12).
3. Screw down the stop nut (20) until it touches the armature plate (3) without gripping it.
4. Repeat the same operation at the three stops.
5. When the adjustment has been carried out, pean the stop nuts into the slots in the studs with the end of a
   screwdriver.

> **NOTE:** Each time the elastic stops are adjusted it is essential that the three complete stop nut
> assemblies (20) are replaced (nut, washer, rubber ring), together with the three lower armature plate
> thrust washers (23).

### Refitting the mechanism (simplified Ferlec 6 V)

<!-- PDF p.100 · C-34 -->

1. Clean the locating faces on the coil housing and the crankshaft.
2. Refit the mechanism, tighten and lock the four bolts.
3. Fit the friction disc with the hub offset towards the crankshaft, followed by the pressure plate without

4. Fit centralising mandrel Emb.02 to locate the friction disc.

6. Remove the mandrel and connect the engine to the gearbox (transmission case) assembly.

### Principles of operation (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

<!-- PDF p.106 · C-41 -->

**Type BHA 8 clutch mechanism.** <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: OCR read the type as "BHA 8"; on the page image the leading letter is an ambiguous typewriter glyph (reads as "EHA" or "BHA"), same as the 6-Volt "BHA 5"/"EHA 5" at C-22. Kept the OCR reading; verify against a cleaner scan. -->
The coil (9) contained in coil housing (1) is fed by two slip rings (7) and (12). Ring (7) is earthed
(grounded). Ring (12) carries current to coil (9) through strip (6). The current flow attracts the movable
"armature plate – pressure plate" assembly, which is attracted towards coil housing (1) and grips friction
disc (4). The engine drive is thus transmitted to the output shaft. If the coil feed current is switched off,
the "armature plate – pressure plate" assembly is no longer attracted, and springs (10) push back this
assembly, thus releasing the friction disc. The drive from the engine is then no longer transmitted to the
output shaft.

![Simplified "Ferlec" 12-Volt clutch — exploded view (parts 1-12) — PDF p.105](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0105-simplified-12v-clutch-exploded.webp)

**Electrical circuit.** The BOSCH dynamo has a high current output at low speeds. To obtain progressive
clutch engagement, this output must be limited. A resistance (2) of 3 ohms has therefore been connected in
series into the dynamo field circuit. When the engine is accelerated, the rheostat cursor frees the
microswitch (5) (inside the case) which cuts out resistance (2). When the engine is decelerated the cam,
which is integral with the rheostat cursor, opens the microswitch (5) and in this way resistance (2) is once
more connected into the circuit.

Either the battery or the dynamo circuit can be used to feed the clutch. The entire system is common to both
methods of feed, except for voltage regulator (11) and resistance (2) which only operates when the dynamo is
providing current.

*Operation in 1st – Reverse.* The gear change lever (17) is in the neutral position and the two-way switch
in the "dynamo" position. Initially resistance (2) limits the output of dynamo (10). When 1st is engaged,
relay (3) closes and the microswitch (8) remains open. As soon as the engine is accelerated cam (6) frees
microswitch (5) which closes and short circuits resistance (2). Current flowing through coil (9) causes it to
attract armature plate (1) and grip friction disc (4). The vehicle moves off.

*Operation in 2nd and 3rd.* When selecting from 1st into 2nd, the lever (17) is brought to the neutral
position and the energising circuit of relay (3) is earthed (grounded). Coil (3) is no longer fed with
current and the friction disc (4) is released. 2nd is then engaged, relay (3) closes, the gear change lever
closes microswitch (8) which short circuits part of the rheostat. Resistance (2) is momentarily connected in
series into the dynamo field circuit. Coil (9) is fed with current and grips friction disc (4). The engine
drives the vehicle.

   the plates (11) to coil housing (1). Remove the armature plate (2). Clean the components with
   trichlorethylene.
4. Fit three new plates (11). Do not tighten nuts (22); the bolts (19) of which should be slit (if they are
   not, replace them).
5. Replace the three thrust washers (23). Position armature plate (2) on coil housing (1). Engage the plates
   (11) in their locations on the coil housing (1). Fit bolts (21) and their washers.
6. Tighten the three bolts (21) and the three nuts (22) to a **torque of 1 m.kg (10 lb/ft)**.

> **NOTE 1.** If there are any taper adjusting shims between the intermediate plate and the coil housing, mark
> their positions and put them aside to be refitted in the same positions during re-assembly.
>
> **NOTE 2.** A set of drive plates can be obtained under the number 8.540.863.

**Machining operations.** Refacing of the intermediate and pressure plates is permitted up to a metal removal
of 0.2 mm (.008"). However, replacement of the intermediate plate or the pressure plate involves replacing
the entire clutch. The machining operations should be very carefully carried out in order to remain within
the dimensional and finish tolerances essential to the efficient operation of this assembly. One must machine
the pressure plate securing lugs by dimension equal to the total thickness removed from the pressure plate
(6) and the intermediate plate (4). The four-bladed and six-bladed friction discs are interchangeable. New
slip rings can be skimmed to ensure that they are perfectly concentric and to remove any buckle. The
crankshaft locating face on the flywheel can also be skimmed to ensure that it is perfectly flat.

### Refitting the mechanism (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

<!-- PDF p.111 · C-46 -->

1. Clean the locating faces of the coil housing and the crankshaft with trichlorethylene. Refit the "coil
   housing – armature plate" assembly, paying attention to its position with respect to the crankshaft.
2. Tighten and lock the 4 crankshaft securing bolts.
3. Fit the intermediate plate (3) — one of the lugs has a chamfered corner which corresponds to the position
   of the coil feed terminal (9). If there are any, refit the taper adjusting shims which were marked during
   dismantling.
4. Clean the slip rings with alcohol. Fit the friction disc (with the offset of the hub towards the
   crankshaft), the air gap adjusting shims (0.2 mm) (.008"), and the pressure plate. Tab (6) aligns with the
   coil feed terminal (9). Connect up tab (6) and lock the screw.
5. Refit the carbon brushes and ensure that they slide freely in their locations. Connect the feed lead to
   the "+" carbon brush. The plug earths (grounds) the negative carbon brush.
6. Fit the friction disc locating mandrel and energise the clutch with a separate 12 volt battery. Remove the
   mandrel and connect the engine to the gear box. Switch off the 12 volt current.

> **NOTE:** Before refitting, it is advisable to re-assemble the mechanism, position it vertically, energise
> it with 12 volts current, and check that it operates correctly.

### Checking the microswitch in the case (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

<!-- PDF p.112 · C-47 -->

Adjust the engine idling speed. Adjust the case linkage and position the rheostat lever by means of special
tool Emb.16. Connect a pilot light fed from the + "Bat." between terminals 7 and 8 (select a pilot light the
current requirements of which do not exceed 1 amp). With no pressure applied to the accelerator, the light
should glow weakly. If it is fully on, the microswitch operating cam must be adjusted. To adjust the operating
cam:

1. Loosen the cam locking screw, adjust sufficiently to permit it to slide (with tool Emb.16 still in place).
2. Slide the cam towards the microswitch until the pilot light starts to dim.
3. Retighten the cam locking screw. Mark the position of the cam on its support with a light scriber line.
4. Loosen the cam locking screw and move the cam from **0.6 to 0.8 mm (.024 to .032")** towards the
   microswitch (**0.6 mm minimum**).

Check that when the engine is accelerated the pilot light switches fully on, and then switches partially off
when the control is released. Reconnect the harness to the case.

> **NOTE:** Initially, the position of the cam is adjusted at the factory, and should not be altered. The
> operating angles are as follows:
> - Angular movement in short circuit: 3°30' maximum (microswitch closed).
> - Angular movement to bring resistance (2) into operation: 2° maximum (microswitch open).

A voltmeter connected between terminals 7 and 8 can be used in place of the pilot light, without
disconnecting the existing wires. The voltmeter shows a voltage when the accelerator is released and no
voltage when the accelerator is pressed.

### Checking the clutch release relay (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

Disconnect lead (6) (violet). Connect a light between terminal (6) and earth (ground). Place the instrument
panel switch in the "battery" position. The gear change lever is in the neutral position. Switch on the
ignition. The light switches on. When a gear is engaged the light should switch off.

### Checking the microswitch under the floor (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

<!-- PDF p.113 · C-48 -->

Disconnect lead (5) (yellow). Connect a light between this lead and earth (ground). (To make checking when
the vehicle is both stationary and moving easier, use the long lead so that the light can be brought round to
the dashboard or hooked to the earth (ground) point provided by the pivoting window locking lever.)

**When stationary.** Place the switch in the "battery" position and operate the lever. The light should:
- remain switched off if the gear box is in 1st or in reverse.
- switch on if the gear box is in 2nd or 3rd.

**When moving.** The switch can be placed in either the "battery" or the "dynamo" position. Drive the car. The
light should:
- remain switched off if the gear box is in 1st or reverse.
- switch on if the gear box is in 2nd or 3rd.

When the gears are engaged check that the lever movement and vehicle vibration do not affect the operation of
the microswitch.

### Checking the gear lever (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

Disconnect leads (1) (pink) and (2) (black). Connect the light between these two leads. Place the dashboard
switch in the "battery" position. When the gears are engaged the light should switch on. As soon as pressure
is removed from the gear change lever, the light should switch off.

### Adjustments — mechanical (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

<!-- PDF p.114 · C-49 -->

**Control linkage.** Adjust the engine idling speed. Place tool Emb.16 against the case. Place the stud on
the tool in the hole in the rheostat lever. The cursor position thus established corresponds to a rheostat
adjustment which provides the correct progressive action during clutch engagement.

**Air gap.** Place the switch under the dashboard in the "battery" position. Measure the air gap J at three
points 120° apart on the coil housing. The air gap should be between **0.20 and 0.41 mm (.008" and .016")**.
To obtain this dimension insert or remove adjusting shims (13). Adjust by means of the adjusting resistance.

**Mechanical adjustment (before refitting).** Refit the mechanism assembly following the "armature plate –
coil housing" reference marks made during dismantling. If a new friction disc is being fitted, insert a new
0.2 mm (.008") shim in each lug. If a worn friction disc is being re-used, insert 0.2 mm (.008") shims as
required. Feed the clutch with 12 volt current. Check that the air gap is constant between 0.20 and 0.41 mm
(.008" and .016").

### Adjusting the armature plate elastic stop (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

<!-- PDF p.115 · C-50 -->

1. Remove the friction disc and refit the pressure plate.
2. Insert a 2.9 mm (.114") spacer near the stop that is to be adjusted and between the pressure and
   intermediate plates.
3. Screw down the stop nut, without tightening it, until it touches the armature plate.
4. Repeat the same operation for the other three stops.
5. After adjusting, pean the stop nuts into the slots in the studs.

> **NOTE:** At each adjustment replace: the three nuts, the washers and the rubber rings; and the three thrust
> washers (23).

### Adjustments — electrical (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

**Adjusting resistance.** As the air gap becomes smaller and smaller, with wear in the friction disc, clutch
engagement becomes more and more violent. To make it more progressive, the strength of the current flowing
through the coil (9) must be reduced, and therefore the strength of resistance (4) must be increased by moving
the clamp towards the right hand side of the vehicle. (Do this by small increments at a time.) If the
electrical adjustment is insufficient, the clamp is to be returned to its initial position. Adjust the air
gap and finish off the adjustment by means of the resistance.

**Microswitch under the floor.** Bring the gear change lever as far as it will go to the 2nd and 3rd speed
side. Secure the microswitch bracket so that the strips just make contact with the gear change lever. Engage
1st or reverse; the microswitch should then be open (electrical check). This adjustment is difficult to carry
out owing to the design of the microswitch bracket. After a certain number of operations, the strips lose
their elasticity and the microswitch remains either permanently open or permanently closed. In this case,
replace the microswitch and its bracket.

### Gear change lever (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

<!-- PDF p.116 · C-51 -->

**Adjusting the silver plated spring (11 coils).** Screw the new silver plated spring (14) on to stud (18) on
the lever so that A = 25.4 −0/−0.5 mm (63/64 to 1"). The upper oval section of the sleeve (16) should have its
longest axis parallel with the longitudinal centre line of the vehicle. Screw the sleeve fully in and return
by a maximum of ½ a turn to obtain the correct position. Tighten the lock nut (19). Reconditioning is limited
to replacing the silver plated spring (14) and the feed lead (see the Ferodo instructions supplied with spare
parts 8.541.098).

### Checking the friction disc (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

Check:
- The evenness of the thickness of the friction disc segments (when they are new this is 2.5 mm (.099")).
  Tolerance ± 0.04 mm (.002").
- The buckle (mandrel Emb.06 and a dial indicator). If the buckle is near the tolerance correct it with fork
  Emb.08. If the buckle is excessive, replace the friction disc.
- The surface finish.

### Checking the pressure plate (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

<!-- PDF p.117 · C-52 -->

Check the plate for bow. Maximum clearance between the straight edge and the plate: 0.04 mm (.002"). If the
clearance is greater than 0.04 mm (.002") replace the pressure and intermediate plates (they may be refaced).

### Checking the intermediate plate (simplified Ferlec 12 V)

Check:
- The buckle, which should not exceed 0.04 mm (.002"). If the buckle is greater than 0.04 mm (.002"), replace
  the plate. (Check the coil housing locating face on the crankshaft. It may be refaced.)
- For bow. Maximum clearance between rule and plate: 0.04 mm (.002"). If the clearance is greater than
  0.04 mm (.002"), the plate may be refaced.
