## A — 5-hole star member wheels

### A.I — Specifications

<!-- PDF p.313 · K-4 -->

**Front.** The front hub runs on taper roller bearings.

**Rear.** The rear hub is splined to the half shaft and secured by a taper washer and a nut. The half

| Item | Value |
| ---- | ----- |
| Initial diameter of drums | 228.5 mm (9") |
| Maximum diameter after re-working | 229.2 mm (9 1/16") |

### A.II — Removing and refitting the front hubs

<!-- PDF p.314 · K-5 -->

**Removing:**

1. Remove the wheel embellisher.
2. Loosen the wheel nuts.
3. Lift the vehicle and remove the wheel.
4. Remove the hub plug by means of tongs Rou.08.
5. Unpin.
6. Remove the nut and washer.
7. Pull back the brake shoes from the drum.
8. Remove the hub-drum, wheel flange assembly by means of extractor B.Tr.02.

**Refitting:**

1. Ensure that the bearings are in good condition.
2. Carry out the removing operations in reverse.
3. Then: tighten the nut, whilst turning the wheel, until a slight stiffness is felt.
4. Loosen the nut by 1/6 of a turn and split-pin it.
5. Tap the end of the stub axle with a mallet to "bed down" the bearing, then fit the hub-plug which
   is previously 3/4 filled with grease.
6. Adjust the brake shoes.

### A.III — Replacing the bearings in one front hub

<!-- PDF p.315 · K-6 -->

![Front hub-drum bearing assembly — exploded view (bolts, bearing stop, baffle washer, bearings, track rings, hub-drum) — PDF p.315](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0315-front-hub-exploded.webp)

**Removing:**

1. Remove the front "hub-drum" assembly.
2. Remove the inner bearing by means of tool B.Vi.28.
3. Remove the bearing, the baffle washer and the bearing stop.
4. Remove the bolts which connect the "flange-hub-drum" assembly together if necessary.
5. Remove the hub bearing track rings.
6. Fit the new track rings whilst ensuring that their locating bores are clean.
7. Assemble the "drum-hub-flange" assembly without tightening the bolts and centralize it on a lathe
   (see [section A.VII](#avii-centralizing-the-drum-hub-flange-assembly-on-a-lathe)).

**Refitting:**

1. The outer chamfer on the inner bearing stop should be against the brake anchor plate.
2. Refit the assembly to the stub axle.
3. Fill the bearings and the face between the two bearing areas with grease.
4. Refit the front "hub-drum" assembly.

> **NOTE:** When refitting a bearing it is essential to replace the corresponding track ring.

### A.IV — Adjusting the front hub bearing play

<!-- PDF p.315 · K-6 -->

1. Ensure that the bearings are in good condition.
2. Tighten the nut whilst turning the wheel until a slight resistance to rotation is felt.
3. Loosen the nut by 1/6 of a turn and split-pin it.
4. Tap the end of the stub axle with a mallet in order to bed down the bearing, then fit the hub plug
   3/4 full of grease.
5. Adjust the brake shoes.

### A.V — Assembling the brake anchor plates to the stub axles

<!-- PDF p.316 · K-7 -->

During manufacture the brake anchor plate is connected to the stub axle by means of rivets. Under
repair conditions it is possible to assemble these parts as shown in the drawing (figures 47762-1 and
47763).

1. Spotface the four outer faces of the stub axle to obtain a bearing area with a radius **r = 11 mm
   (7/16")** and a thickness **A = 7 mm (9/32")**. These faces must be perfectly parallel with the
   thrust face of the brake anchor plate.
2. Use a spotface fitted with a special drive and guide (Ref. 12.275). Use the spotface in a drilling
   press which has a fixed pillar. Take a very light cut at the beginning of the spotfacing operation.
3. Connect the plate to the stub axle by means of shouldered bolts only (bolts used to secure the
   750 cc crown wheel), with the heads of the bolts on the brake anchor plate side.
4. Place a type A 8D steel flat washer with an inside diameter of **8 mm (21/64")** under each bolt
   head (this washer must be of a slightly larger diameter than that of the shoulder on the bolt).
5. Place a shakeproof washer (W8) under the H 8D steel nut.
6. Tighten down the nuts alternately.

### A.VI — Replacing a front drum or hub

<!-- PDF p.317 · K-8 -->

#### Replacing a drum (5-hole)

1. Remove the front "hub-drum" assemblies.
2. Remove the bolts which secure the "flange-hub-drum" assembly.

New drums are supplied by the C.S.S. rough bored to a diameter of **227 mm (8 15/16")**. They must
therefore be re-bored after they are fitted to the hub and after the "hub-drum-flange" assembly has
been centralized.

However, to ensure that an evenly balanced braking effect is obtained, both drums on any given axle
must be:

- of the same diameter;
- of identical surface finishes.

It is therefore essential that the operation should be carried out in the following manner:

1. Re-work (by removing the minimum of material) the drum which is to be retained (maximum grinding
   diameter **229.2 mm (9 1/16")**).
2. Measure the diameter after re-working and re-work the new drum to the same diameter.
3. If both drums are to be replaced, re-work them to the initial diameter, that is to say **228.5 mm
   (9")**.
4. Refit the "front-hub-drum" assemblies.

#### Replacing a hub (5-hole)

1. Remove the "hub-drum" assemblies.
2. Remove the bolts which connect the "drum-hub-flange" assembly together.
3. Replace the hub.
4. Reassemble the "flange-drum-hub" assembly and centralize it.
5. If the eccentricity of the drum is greater than **0.05 mm (.002")** re-work both the drums on a
   lathe.
6. Refit the "hub-drum" assemblies.

> Under no circumstances should the re-working diameter be greater than 229.2 mm (9") <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: source misprint, not OCR — page image prints "229.2 mm (9")" here, but 229.2 mm is given as (9 1/16") everywhere else in this chapter (K-4, K-8 left column, K-12, K-17). The imperial "(9")" is inconsistent with the metric value. -->. If it is, the drum is to be replaced.

### A.VII — Centralizing the drum-hub-flange assembly on a lathe

<!-- PDF p.317 · K-8 -->

**Front or rear.**

1. Loosen the bolts which connect the "flange-hub-drum" assembly.
2. Mount the assembly between centres on the lathe.
   - On the front, the bearing track rings must be in position in the hub.
   - At the rear, the half shaft must be removed.
3. Centralize by means of a height gauge, taking as reference points:
   - the rear studs for the flange;
   - the brake shoe friction surface for the drum.
4. After centralizing: tighten the bolts, opposite one another and alternately.

> **NOTE:** It is essential to centre up in all cases involving replacing a flange or an "hub-drum"
> assembly.

### A.VIII — Removing and refitting a rear hub

<!-- PDF p.318 · K-9 -->

**Removing:**

1. Loosen the wheel nuts.
2. Pull back the shoes from the drum (by means of the adjusting squares).
3. Remove the bearing cover securing nuts.
4. Knock the wheel in order to free the "hub-drum-half shaft" assembly. It is forbidden to tap on the
   studs in order to avoid knocking the seal out of its cage (during assembly this seal is fitted to
   the cage on a press).

> **NOTE:** In cases where the half shaft is broken and difficulties encountered in removing the
> splined end, remove the half shaft tube.

**Removing a half shaft:**

1. Remove the hub nut in a vice.
2. Use support (Rou.11). Tightening torque for the hub nut, **22 to 25 m.kg (145 to 185 lbs.ft)** <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: source misprint, not OCR — page image prints "22 to 25 m.kg"; every other statement of this torque (K-11, K-20, K-21) prints "20 to 25 m.kg", and the (145 to 185 lbs.ft) conversion corresponds to 20–25 m.kg, so "22" appears to be a misprint for "20". Kept as printed. -->.
3. Remove the half shaft on the press.

**Refitting:**

1. Ensure that the bearing is in good condition and grease it.
2. Refit the half shaft on the hub.
3. Engage the shaft into the half shaft tube, with the flat on the bearing cover upwards, in order to
   clear the hand brake link.
4. Insert the studs into the brake anchor plate. Fit the shakeproof washers.
5. Screw up and tighten the nuts. Tightening torque **5 m.kg (40 lbs.ft)**.
6. Adjust the brake shoes.

### A.IX — Replacing a rear hub bearing and seal

<!-- PDF p.319 · K-10 -->

![Rear hub bearing and seal — exploded view (46 262) and bearing-cover cross-section (46 249 A) — PDF p.319](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0319-rear-hub-bearing-exploded.webp)

**Removing:** remove the hub, the half shaft and the bearing cover. Check to ensure that the
"drum - hub - flange" assembly is central.

**Replacing the bearing:**

1. Extract the bearing from the shaft on the press.
2. Refit the new, greased bearing on the half shaft on the press.

**Replacing the "Paulstra" seal and the paper gasket.**

> It is essential that the bearing cover spacers on the studs should be accurately positioned when
> reassembling. This is to avoid damaging the bearing.

Only the operations involved in replacing the "Paulstra" seal or replacing the bearing cover paper
gasket involve removing the studs and their spacers:

1. Loosen the studs with a nut and a lock nut and unscrew them.
2. Push out the spacers on the press.

<!-- PDF p.320 · K-11 -->

**Then refitting:**

1. Tighten the studs into the bearing cover.
2. Replace the "Paulstra" seal, fitting it the correct way round (with its lip towards the bearing)
   and the paper gasket.
3. Push in the spacers on the press over the serrations on the studs, taking care to place flats "A"
   on the shaft side.
4. Reassemble the half shaft and its cover to the hub on the press (tightening torque for the nut
   **20 to 25 m.kg (145 to 185 lbs/ft)**).
5. Place the shaft into the half shaft tube with the flat on the bearing cover upwards in order to
   clear the hand brake link.
6. Insert the studs into the brake anchor plate. Fit the shake-proof washers.
7. Screw in and tighten the nuts. Tightening torque **5 m.kg (40 lbs/ft)**.
8. Adjust the brake shoes.

### A.X — Replacing a rear drum or hub

<!-- PDF p.320 · K-11 -->

**Replacing the drum:**

1. Remove the rear "hub - drum" assembly.
2. Remove the half shaft.
3. Remove the bolts which connect the "flange - hub - drum" assembly together.
4. Carry out the same operations as are involved in [replacing a front drum](#avi-replacing-a-front-drum-or-hub).
5. Refit the "hub - drum" assemblies.

**Replacing a hub:**

1. Remove the "hub - drum" assemblies.
2. Remove the half shaft.
3. Remove the bolts which connect the "flange - hub - drum" together.
4. Carry out the same operations as are involved in [replacing a front hub](#avi-replacing-a-front-drum-or-hub).
5. Refit the "hub-drum" assemblies.

### B.VI — Replacing a front hub or drum

<!-- PDF p.326 · K-17 -->

> **IMPORTANT:** The width of the braking surface for 30 mm (1 3/16") shoes was originally 39 mm
> (1 9/16"). Since then the width has been increased to 46 mm (1 13/16"). The drums supplied by the
> C.S.S. also have a braking area width of 46 mm (1 13/16"). However, you must ensure that you do not
> fit drums that you may have in stock with a braking area width of 39 mm (1 9/16") to a car fitted
> with brake shoes 35 mm (1 13/32") wide. Furthermore, under no circumstances must shoes 30 mm
> (1 3/16") wide be replaced by shoes 35 mm (1 13/32") wide.

#### Replacing a drum (3-hole)

1. Remove the front "hub - drum" assemblies.
2. Separate the hub from the drum by pushing out the three assembly bolts on the press.

The new drums are supplied by the C.S.S. rough bored to a diameter of **227 mm (8 15/16")**. They
must therefore be rebored after they are mounted on the hubs.

However, to maintain even braking effect it is essential that both the drums on any given axle are:

- of the same diameter;
- have identical surface finishes.

Consequently, it is essential that the operations should be carried out in the following way:

1. Rework (removing a minimum of material) the drum which is to be retained (maximum reworking
   diameter **229.2 mm (9 1/16")**).
2. Measure the diameter after reworking and rework the new drum to the same diameter.
3. If both the drums are to be replaced, rework them to the initial diameter, that is to say **228.5
   mm (9")**.
4. Refit the front "hub - drum" assemblies.

#### Replacing a hub (3-hole)

1. Remove the "hub - drum" assemblies.
2. Separate the hub from the drum by pushing out the three connecting bolts on the press.
3. Replace the hub.
4. Assemble the "hub - drum", after inserting washers, pressing in the three connecting bolts on the
   press.
5. Check the drum for eccentricity. If the eccentricity is greater than **0.05 mm (.002")**, rework
   both drums on a lathe.

> Under no circumstances must the reworking operation be to a greater diameter than 229.2 mm
> (9 1/16"). If it is, the drum is to be replaced.

<!-- PDF p.327 · K-18 -->

> **IMPORTANT — Two types of drum have been fitted since these vehicles were introduced:**
>
> | Feature | Type 1 | Type 2 |
> | ------- | ------ | ------ |
> | Braking area width | 39 mm (1 9/16") | 46 mm (1 13/16") |
> | Width of shoes | 30 mm (1 3/16") | 35 mm (1 13/16") <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: source misprint, not OCR — page image prints the 35 mm shoe as "35 mm (1 13/16")"; the 35 mm shoe is given as (1 13/32") everywhere else in this chapter (K-16, K-17), and (1 13/16") is the braking-area figure in the row above. "1 13/16"" appears to be a misprint for "1 13/32"". Kept as printed. --> |
>
> Consequently one must ensure that the shoes and the drums correspond one with the other. Refit the
> "hub - drum" assemblies.

> **NOTE:** The "hub - drums" fitted to the R.1093 are the same type as those fitted in production to
> the R.1090 - R.1091. However, in order to improve their performance, the drums fitted to the front
> wheels of the R.1093 had cooling fins on the outside.

### B.VII — Replacing the hub securing bolts

<!-- PDF p.327 · K-18 -->

1. Remove the hub.
2. Mark the relative positions of the hub and the drum.
3. Extract the three bolts taking the load on a tube in order to avoid distorting the drum.
4. Re-assemble the hub and the drum following the position marks made during dismantling, after

5. Tighten the assembly together with a spacer and a nut (tightening torque **10 to 12 m.kg (75

<!-- PDF p.328 · K-19 -->

1. Place the spring on the drum behind the two bolts and fit it with a screwdriver.

### B.IX — Removing and refitting a rear hub

<!-- PDF p.328 · K-19 -->

**Removing:**

1. Loosen the wheel nuts.
2. Pull back the shoes from the drum (by means of the adjusting squares).
3. Remove the bearing cover securing nuts.
4. Tap on the wheel to free the "hub - drum - half shaft" assembly. It is forbidden to strike the
   studs in order to avoid removing the seal from its cage (on fitting, this seal is fitted to the
   cage on a press).

> **NOTE:** If the half shaft breaks and difficulty is encountered in removing the splined end,
> remove the half shaft tube.

<!-- PDF p.329 · K-20 -->

**Removing a half shaft:**

1. Loosen the hub nut in the vice.
2. Use support (Rou.11). Hub nut tightening torque **20 to 25 m.kg (145 to 185 lbs/ft)**.
3. Remove the half shaft on the press.

**Refitting:**

1. Ensure that the bearing is in good condition and grease it.
2. Check the condition of the paper gasket and replace it if necessary.
3. Refit the half shaft to the hub.
4. Insert the shaft into the half shaft tube, with the flat on the bearing cover facing upwards, to
   clear the handbrake link.
5. Insert the studs into the brake anchor plate.
6. Fit the shakeproof washers.
7. Screw in and tighten the nuts (tightening torque **5 m.kg (40 lbs/ft)**).
8. Adjust the brake shoes.

### B.X — Replacing a rear hub bearing and seal

<!-- PDF p.330 · K-21 -->

![Rear hub bearing and seal cross-section (51928-2) showing nuts B, nut C and nylon-cage bearing orientation A — PDF p.330](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0330-rear-hub-bearing-section.webp)

1. Remove the "hub - drum - half shaft" assembly.
2. Separate the parts.

**Replacing the bearing and seal:**

The seal is fitted to the flange on the press with its lip towards the bearing.

| Fastener | Tightening torque |
| -------- | ----------------- |
| Nuts (B) | 5 m.kg (40 lbs/ft) |
| Nuts (C) | 20 - 25 m.kg (145 to 185 lbs/ft) |

> **NOTE:** Smear the bearing with grease before refitting.

Place the following on the shaft:

- the bearing;
- the bearing cover fitted with a seal and a paper gasket.

> When fitting a bearing with a nylon cage, position it so that the balls can be seen from the
> threaded end of the shaft — that is to say that the full disc of the nylon cage (A) is on the
> universal joint side.

Place the shaft into the hub and fit the split ring. Tighten nut (C) and split-pin it. Refit the
"hub - drum - half shaft" assembly.

### B.XI — Replacing a rear drum or hub

<!-- PDF p.331 · K-22 -->

**Replacing a drum:**

1. Remove the "hub - drum - half shaft" assemblies.
2. Separate the parts.
3. Carry out the same operations as were involved in [replacing a front drum](#bvi-replacing-a-front-hub-or-drum).
4. Refit the "hub - drum - half shaft" assemblies.

**Replacing a hub:**

1. Remove the "hub - drum - half shaft" assemblies.
2. Separate the parts.
3. Carry out the same operations as were involved in [replacing a front hub](#bvi-replacing-a-front-hub-or-drum).
4. Refit the "hub - drum - half shaft" assemblies.

### B.XII — Checking for eccentricity and buckle

<!-- PDF p.331 · K-22 -->

![Wheel-rim buckle and eccentricity check — dial-indicator points V (buckle) and E / E1 (eccentricity) on the rim section — PDF p.331](https://github.com/spacecowboyian/oio-shop-shelf/releases/download/manuals-dauphine/p0331-buckle-eccentricity-gauge.webp)

> A rim must never be checked for buckle and eccentricity when it is fitted with a tyre and on the
> vehicle. Place the stripped wheel on the drive plate of a wheel balancer. The measurements are
> taken by means of dial indicators mounted on the wheel balancer body.

**1. Checking for buckle.** Apply a dial indicator at point V. Tolerance: **1.5 mm (1/16")**.

**2. Checking the eccentricity.** This operation involves using two dial indicators simultaneously.
Apply the dial indicators at points E. The average of the two dimensions measured at any given time
should not exceed **1.8 mm (5/64")**.

Example:

| Reading | Value |
| ------- | ----- |
| E | 1 mm (3/64") |
| E1 | 2 mm (3/32") |
| Eccentricity = (E + E1) / 2 | 1.5 mm (1/16") |

This wheel is therefore acceptable.

### B.XIII — Tyres

<!-- PDF p.332 · K-23 -->

> When fitting the tyres, place the "light point" marking in line with the valve.

| Item | Value |
| ---- | ----- |
| Wheel dimensions | 4 J x 15, three point fastening |
| Tyre dimensions | 145 x 380 or 5.5 x 15 |
| Inflation pressure — front wheels | 1 kg (14 psi) |
| Inflation pressure — rear wheels | 1.5 kg (23 psi) |

In the case of a puncture, the operation of balancing the "wheel - tyre" is to be carried out after
repair.

Every **5,000 km (3,000 miles)**, switch the wheels.
