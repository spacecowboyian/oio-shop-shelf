<a id="p171"></a>
**[PDF p.171]** — DESCRIPTION

This engine (4A-FE shown) uses a **pressurized forced-circulation cooling system** with a
thermostat that has a by-pass valve, mounted on the inlet side. The system comprises the
water jacket (in the cylinder block and head), radiator, water pump, thermostat, electric
fan, hoses and other components.

---
<a id="p172"></a>
**[PDF p.172]** — DESCRIPTION (cont.)

- **Radiator** — cools the coolant from the water jacket; upper and lower tanks joined by a
  core. A/T models include an ATF cooler built into the lower tank. An electric fan behind
  the radiator assists airflow and runs when coolant temperature becomes high.
- **Reservoir tank** — catches coolant that overflows from volumetric expansion; coolant
  returns to the radiator when temperature drops, keeping the radiator full.
- **Water pump** — forced circulation; mounted on the front of the cylinder block, driven
  by the timing belt.
- **Thermostat** — wax-type by-pass valve in the water inlet housing; **operates at 82°C
  (180°F)**. Closes when cold (fast warm-up), opens when hot.
- **Radiator cap** — pressure-type; prevents boiling above **100°C (212°F)**. Relief
  (pressurization) valve vents steam through the overflow pipe when system pressure exceeds
  the limit (coolant temp **110–120°C, 230–248°F**; pressure **0.3–1.0 kg/cm² (4.3–14.2
  psi, 29.4–98.1 kPa)**). A vacuum valve relieves the vacuum that forms after shutdown.

<a id="p174"></a>
**[PDF p.174]** — CHECK AND REPLACEMENT OF ENGINE COOLANT

1. **CHECK ENGINE COOLANT LEVEL AT RESERVE TANK** — level should be between "LOW" and "FULL". If low, check for leaks and add coolant to "FULL".
2. **CHECK ENGINE COOLANT QUALITY** — no excessive rust/scale around the radiator cap or filler hole; coolant free from oil. If excessively dirty, replace.
3. **REPLACE ENGINE COOLANT**
   a. Remove the radiator cap.
      > **CAUTION:** To avoid being burned, do not remove the cap while the engine and radiator are still hot — fluid and steam can be blown out under pressure.
   b. Drain coolant from the radiator and engine drain cocks. (The engine drain cock is near the oil filter.)
   c. Close the drain cocks.
      **Torque (engine drain cock): 350 kg-cm (25 ft-lb, 34 N·m)**
   d. Fill with coolant. Use a good ethylene-glycol antifreeze (or TOYOTA radiator conditioner / equivalent anticorrosive), mixed per the maker's directions.
      > **NOTICE:** Do not use alcohol-type coolant. Mix the coolant with demineralized or distilled water.

      **Capacity (w/ Heater), 4A-FE:** AE M/T **6.2 liters (6.5 US qts, 5.5 Imp. qts)** *(other model/transmission capacities are in the Service Specifications section)*.
   e. Install the radiator cap.
   f. Start the engine and check for leaks.
   g. Recheck the coolant level and refill as necessary.

**[PDF p.175]** — WATER PUMP — COMPONENTS · REMOVAL

Components (exploded view — image on [PDF p.175]): Water Pump Pulley, Pulley Seat, Seal ✦,
Water Pump Suction Cover, O-Ring ✦, Rotor ✦, Gasket ✦, Water Pump Body. (✦ = non-reusable.)

### REMOVAL OF WATER PUMP
1. DRAIN COOLANT (see [PDF p.174]).
2. REMOVE DRIVE BELT AND WATER PUMP PULLEY.
3. REMOVE No.3 AND No.2 TIMING BELT COVERS — 4A-FE (see EM steps on EM-60, EM-62).
4. REMOVE WATER INLET PIPE — disconnect the water inlet and by-pass hoses from the inlet pipe.

---
<a id="p176"></a>
**[PDF p.176]** — WATER PUMP — REMOVAL (cont.)

4. (cont.) Remove the two nuts and clamp bolt; remove the water inlet pipe and O-ring.
5. REMOVE OIL DIPSTICK GUIDE AND DIPSTICK — remove the dipstick; remove the bolt and pull out the dipstick guide (plug the guide hole in the oil pump); remove the O-ring from the guide.
6. REMOVE WATER PUMP — remove the three bolts and the water pump; remove the O-ring.
   > **NOTICE:** Be careful not to get coolant on the timing belt.

### INSPECTION OF WATER PUMP
- Turn the pulley and check that the water pump bearing moves smoothly and quietly.

<a id="p177"></a>
**[PDF p.177]** — WATER PUMP — DISASSEMBLY

1. REMOVE WATER PUMP SUCTION COVER — remove the three bolts; pry off the suction cover with a screwdriver.
2. REMOVE PULLEY SEAT — using SST and a press, remove the pulley seat from the bearing shaft. **SST 09236-00101 (09237-00010, 09237-00040)**
3. REMOVE WATER PUMP BEARING
   a. Using a grinding wheel, grind the water pump rotor as shown.
      > **NOTICE:** Do not grind the water pump bearing shaft.
   b. Heat the water pump body to approx. **85°C (185°F)**.
   c. Using SST and a press, remove the rotor and bearing. **SST 09236-00101 (09237-00010, 09237-00040)**
   d. Remove the seal set from the body.
      > **NOTICE:** Do not damage the water pump body.
4. REMOVE SEAL — using SST and a press, remove the seal. **SST 09236-00101 (09236-15010)**

---
<a id="p178"></a>
**[PDF p.178]** — WATER PUMP — ASSEMBLY

> **HINT:** Always assemble the water pump with a new seal set, a new rotor and a new bearing.

1. INSTALL WATER PUMP BEARING — gradually heat the body to approx. **85°C (185°F)**; using SST and a press, install a new bearing. **SST 09236-00101 (09237-00020)**
   > **HINT:** The bearing end face should be flush with the body top surface.
2. INSTALL SEAL
   a. Apply seal packing No.1282-B to a new seal and the pump body. **Seal packing: Part No. 08826-00100 or equivalent**
   b. Using SST and a press, install the seal on the bearing. **SST 09236-00101 (09237-00020)**
3. INSTALL PULLEY SEAT — using SST and a press, install the pulley seat on the bearing shaft. **SST 09236-00101 (09237-00020)**
   > **HINT:** Distance from the pulley seat to the pump body installation surface — **Seat distance: 76.7 mm (3.020 in.)**

---
<a id="p179"></a>
**[PDF p.179]** — WATER PUMP — ASSEMBLY (cont.)

4. INSTALL ROTOR
   a. Install a new packing and seat into the rotor.
   b. Apply a little LLC to the seal and rotor contact surface.
   c. Using a press, install a new rotor on the bearing shaft.
      > **HINT:** Distance from the rotor edge to the pump body installation surface should be **6.0 mm (0.236 in.)**.
5. CHECK WATER PUMP — after assembly, make sure the rotor rotates smoothly.
6. INSTALL WATER PUMP SUCTION COVER — install on a new gasket with three bolts.
   **Torque: 92.5 kg-cm (80 in.-lb, 9.1 N·m)**
   > **HINT:** After installing, make sure the rotor does not contact the suction cover.

<a id="p180"></a>
**[PDF p.180]** — WATER PUMP — INSTALLATION

1. INSTALL WATER PUMP
   a. Place a new O-ring on the cylinder block.
   b. Install the water pump with the three bolts. **Torque: 150 kg-cm (11 ft-lb, 15 N·m)**
2. INSTALL OIL DIPSTICK GUIDE AND DIPSTICK
   a. Install a new O-ring to the guide.
   b. Apply soapy water to the O-ring.
   c. Push in the guide and install with the bolt. **Torque: 95 kg-cm (82 in.-lb, 9.3 N·m)**
3. INSTALL WATER INLET PIPE
   a. Place a new O-ring on the water pump.
   b. Temporarily install with the two nuts and bolt.
   c. Tighten the two nuts. **Torque: 200 kg-cm (14 ft-lb, 20 N·m)**
   d. Tighten the bolt. **Torque: 130 kg-cm (9 ft-lb, 13 N·m)**

---
<a id="p181"></a>
**[PDF p.181]** — WATER PUMP — INSTALLATION (cont.)

3. (cont.) e. Connect the water inlet and by-pass hoses to the inlet pipe (4A-FE).
4. INSTALL No.2 AND No.3 TIMING BELT COVERS — 4A-FE (see EM steps on EM-86, EM-88).
5. TEMPORARILY INSTALL WATER PUMP PULLEY.
6. INSTALL ALTERNATOR DRIVE BELT.

<a id="p182"></a>
**[PDF p.182]** — THERMOSTAT — REMOVAL · INSPECTION · INSTALLATION

### REMOVAL OF THERMOSTAT
1. DRAIN ENGINE COOLANT (see [PDF p.174]).
2. REMOVE WATER INLET AND THERMOSTAT
   a. Remove the **two nuts (4A-FE)** or **two bolts (4A-GE)** and the water inlet from the water inlet housing.
   b. Remove the thermostat.
   c. Remove the gasket from the thermostat.

### INSPECTION OF THERMOSTAT
> **HINT:** The thermostat is numbered with the valve opening temperature.

a. Immerse the thermostat in water and gradually heat the water.
b. Check the valve opening temperature. **Valve opening temperature: 80–84°C (176–183°F)** — if not as specified, replace.
c. Check the valve lift. **Valve lift: 8 mm (0.31 in.) or more at 95°C (203°F)** — if less than spec, replace.

### INSTALLATION OF THERMOSTAT
1. PLACE THERMOSTAT IN WATER PUMP
   a. Install a new gasket to the thermostat.
   b. **(4A-FE Type A)** Set the jiggle valve in the angle position and insert the thermostat in the water inlet housing.
      > **HINT:** The jiggle valve may be set within 10° of either side of the prescribed position.

---
<a id="p183"></a>
**[PDF p.183]** — THERMOSTAT — INSTALLATION (cont.)

1. (cont.) **(4A-FE Type B)** Align the jiggle valve with the upper side of the stud bolt and insert the thermostat in the water inlet housing (jiggle valve within 10° either side).
2. INSTALL WATER INLET — install with the two nuts (4A-FE). **Torque: 95 kg-cm (82 in.-lb, 9.3 N·m)**
3. FILL WITH ENGINE COOLANT (see [PDF p.174]).
4. START ENGINE AND CHECK FOR LEAKS.

<a id="p184"></a>
**[PDF p.184]** — RADIATOR — CLEANING · INSPECTION

### CLEANING OF RADIATOR
- Using water or a steam cleaner, remove mud and dirt from the radiator core.
  > **NOTICE:** With a high-pressure cleaner, do not deform the core fins. At a nozzle
  > pressure of **30–35 kg/cm² (427–498 psi, 2,942–3,432 kPa)**, keep at least
  > **40–50 cm (15.75–19.69 in.)** between the core and the nozzle.

### INSPECTION OF RADIATOR
1. INSPECT RADIATOR CAP — using a radiator cap tester, pump until the relief valve opens. Check that the valve opens between **0.75 kg/cm² (10.7 psi, 74 kPa)** and **1.05 kg/cm² (14.9 psi, 103 kPa)**. Check that the gauge does not drop rapidly while cap pressure is below **0.6 kg/cm² (8.5 psi, 59 kPa)**. If either check is out of limits, replace the cap.
2. CHECK COOLING SYSTEM FOR LEAKS
   a. Fill the radiator with coolant and attach the cap tester.
   b. Warm up the engine.
   c. Pump to **1.2 kg/cm² (17 psi, 118 kPa)** and check that pressure does not drop. If it drops, check hoses, radiator, water pump; if no external leaks, check the heater core, block and head.

<a id="p186"></a>
**[PDF p.186]** — ELECTRIC COOLING FAN — ON-VEHICLE INSPECTION

**Low coolant temperature (below 83°C / 181°F):**
1. TURN IGNITION SWITCH ON — check the fan does **not** rotate. If it rotates, check the fan relay and temperature switch, and for a separated connector or severed wire between them.
2. DISCONNECT TEMPERATURE SWITCH CONNECTOR — check the fan **rotates**. If not, check the fan relay, fan motor, ignition relay and fuse, and for a short between the fan relay and temperature switch.
3. CONNECT TEMPERATURE SWITCH CONNECTOR.

**High coolant temperature (above 93°C / 199°F):**
4. START ENGINE
   a. Raise coolant temperature above **93°C (199°F)**.
   b. Check the fan rotates.
   c. Check the fan stops when coolant temperature is below **83°C (181°F)**. If not, replace the temperature switch.

---
<a id="p187"></a>
**[PDF p.187]** — ELECTRIC COOLING FAN — INSPECTION (temperature switch, engine main relay)

1. INSPECT TEMPERATURE SWITCH — location: on the water inlet housing.
   a. Using an ohmmeter, check **no continuity** when coolant temperature is above **93°C (199°F)**.
   b. Check **continuity** when coolant temperature is below **83°C (181°F)**.
   - If not as specified, replace the switch.
2. INSPECT ENGINE MAIN RELAY — location: engine compartment relay box.
   - **A. Continuity:** continuity between terminals 1–3; continuity between 2–4; no continuity between 4–5. If not as specified, replace.
   - **B. Operation:** apply battery voltage across terminals 1–3; check continuity between 4–5; check no continuity between 2–4. If not as specified, replace.
3. INSPECT COOLING FAN RELAY — location: engine compartment relay box.

---
<a id="p188"></a>
**[PDF p.188]** — ELECTRIC COOLING FAN — INSPECTION (fan relay, fan motor)

- **Cooling fan relay — A. Continuity:** continuity between terminals 1–2; continuity between 3–4. If not as specified, replace.
- **Cooling fan relay — B. Operation:** apply battery voltage across terminals 1–2; check **no** continuity between terminals 3 and 4. If not as specified, replace.
- **4. INSPECT FAN MOTOR** — connect a battery and ammeter to the fan motor connector; check the motor rotates smoothly and current is as specified. **Standard amperage: 2WD 3.2–4.4 A.** If not as specified, replace the cooling fan motor.
