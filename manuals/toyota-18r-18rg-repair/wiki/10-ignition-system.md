# Ignition System

*Section code: `10` | PDF pages 258-284 of `source.pdf` | 27 pages | printed section pages 10-1 … 10-27*

> Faithful transcription. Covers the ignition system circuit, the **18R** distributor
> (breaker-point type) and the **18R-G** distributor (twin-plate breaker), plus the
> ignition coil, high tension cord, and spark plug. This chapter's OCR text layer was
> relatively clean, and **every** gap/clearance/angle/timing/resistance value below was
> **image-verified** against its source page. Two coil resistances the OCR text had
> garbled (primary and external-resistor) were corrected from the page image — see the
> inline notes on [PDF p.282]. The 18R and 18R-G distributor sections are kept separate:
> note the 18R distributor breaks down into **8** numbered components while the 18R-G
> distributor lists **6**, and their ignition timing differs (7° BTDC vs. 12° BTDC).
> See [llm-instructions.md](llm-instructions.md) and the [glossary](../../../skills/auto-mechanic/glossary.md).

### Distributor (18R) — Installation

1. Set No. 1 cylinder to **7° BTDC/compression**. Align the timing mark with the pointer
   (Fig. 10-27). At this time, rocker arms on No. 1 cylinder should be loose and rockers
   on No. 4 should be tight.
2. Set the oil pump shaft slot in direction as shown (Fig. 10-28).
3. Before inserting the distributor, position the rotor and diaphragm as shown (Fig. 10-29).
4. When fully installed, rotor should point toward as shown (Fig. 10-30).

---
<a id="p271"></a>
**[PDF p.271]** — DISTRIBUTOR (18R) — Installation (cont.) · printed page 10-14

Installation (continued):

5. Turn ignition switch to ON position (Fig. 10-31).

   > **CAUTION:** Do not turn the starter motor.

6. Rotate the distributor body counterclockwise until a spark jumps between the points,
   and tighten the clamp bolt in that position (Fig. 10-32).
7. Check ignition timing in idling condition (Fig. 10-33):

   | Item | Value |
   | --- | --- |
   | Ignition timing | 7° BTDC |

   If necessary, align the timing marks by turning the distributor body.

### Distributor (18R-G) — Installation

1. Set No. 1 cylinder to **12° BTDC/compression**. Align the timing mark with the pointer
   (Fig. 10-54).
2. At this time, intake and exhaust valve lifter on No. 1 cylinder should be rotate
   (loose) and valve lifters on No. 4 should be tight (Fig. 10-55).
3. Before inserting the distributor, position the rotor and diaphragm as shown (Fig. 10-56).
4. When fully installed, rotor should point toward as shown (Fig. 10-57).

---
<a id="p281"></a>
**[PDF p.281]** — DISTRIBUTOR (18R-G) — Installation (cont.) · printed page 10-24

Installation (continued):

5. Turn ignition switch to ON position (Fig. 10-58).

   > **CAUTION:** Do not turn the starter motor.

6. Rotate the distributor body counterclockwise until just sparking between the points,
   then tighten the clamp bolt in that position (Fig. 10-59).
7. Check ignition timing in idling condition (Fig. 10-60):

   | Condition | Ignition timing |
   | --- | --- |
   | Idling | 12° BTDC |
   | At coolant below 60°C | 20° BTDC |

   If necessary, align the timing marks by turning the distributor body.

### Ignition Coil — Inspection

1. Clean the coil and inspect it for carbon paths around the terminals, and check the
   outside body for cracks (Fig. 10-61).
2. Inspect the high tension cord insertion hole for carbon deposit or corrosion.
3. Measure the following resistances. If the reading is not within the specified
   resistance, replace the coil (Figs. 10-62, 10-63, 10-64):

   | Measurement | Value |
   | --- | --- |
   | Primary coil resistance (reference only) | 1.3–1.6 Ω <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: OCR text read "13-162"; page image clearly shows "1.3 – 1.6 Ω" — corrected from image --> |
   | Secondary coil resistance (reference only) | 10.2–13.8 kΩ |
   | External resistor resistance (reference only) | 1.3–1.5 Ω <!-- NEEDS REVIEW: OCR text read "13-152"; page image clearly shows "1.3 – 1.5 Ω" — corrected from image --> |

### High Tension Cord

> **CAUTION:**
> 1. Remove high tension cords carefully by pulling the rubber boot (Fig. 10-66).
> 2. Do not bend the cords as the conductors may break (Fig. 10-67).

**Inspection**

1. Check the condition of the cord terminal (Fig. 10-68). If any terminal is corroded,
   clean it, and if it is broken or distorted, replace the cord.

### Spark Plug — Inspection

Inspect for the following items (Figs. 10-70, 10-71). Clean or replace plugs if necessary:

1. Cracks or damages in the threads or insulator.
2. Damaged or deteriorated gaskets.
3. Wear on the electrodes.
4. Burnt condition of electrode and amount of carbon deposit.

### Spark Plug — Gap Adjustment

Check the plug gap with a plug gap gauge (Fig. 10-72). If not to the specified value,
adjust by bending the ground (outer) electrode.

| Item | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Spark plug gap | 0.7–0.8 mm (0.028–0.031 in) |
