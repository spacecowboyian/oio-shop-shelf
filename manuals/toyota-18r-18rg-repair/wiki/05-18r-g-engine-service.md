*Section code: `5` | PDF pages 110-153 of `source.pdf` | 44 pages | printed section pages 5-1 … 5-44*

> Faithful transcription of the twin-cam **18R-G** engine service section. Covers the
> **Cutaway View**, **Cylinder Head** (DOHC — dual camshafts, valve/spring/lifter, valve
> clearance adjustment via selective shim pads), and **Timing Chain** (No.1 and No.2 chains,
> tensioners, dampers/slipper, pump drive shaft and bearing, crankshaft front oil seal). The
> **Cylinder Block** is not repeated here — the manual points to the 16R · 18R section (see
> [PDF p.110](#p110)). All clearance/limit/torque/diameter specs were **image-verified**
> against the page scans. The OCR text layer dropped or garbled several digits — corrected
> from the images and noted inline: exhaust valve-head margin **0.6 mm** (not 0.8), head-bolt
> torque **52.1–63.7 ft·lb** (not 62.1), exhaust valve clearance **0.29–0.39 mm** (not
> 0.29–0.38 / 0.23–0.33), pump-drive-shaft gear wear limit **114.5 mm**, No.1-chain camshaft
> mounting torque **50.6–57.8 ft·lb** (not 60.6), and the intake/exhaust shim-pad formula
> constants **0.29 / 0.34 mm**. See [llm-instructions.md](llm-instructions.md).

## Cylinder Head — Inspection & Repair

### Cylinder Head

1. Clean the combustion chamber and remove any gasket material from the manifold and head
   surface. Check the cylinder heads for cracks or excessively burnt valve surfaces.
2. Check the cylinder head surface flatness with a precision straight edge.
3. If warpage exceeds the limit, correct it by machining, or replace the head.

   | Item | Value |
   | ---- | ----- |
   | Head surface warpage limit | 0.05 mm (0.002 in) |
   | Maximum reface limit | 0.2 mm (0.008 in) |

### Valve, Guide and Seat

1. Clean valves.

---
<a id="p120"></a>
**[PDF p.120]** — CYLINDER HEAD — Valve, Guide and Seat (cont.) · printed page 5-11

2. Check the valve stem to valve guide clearance of each valve by inserting the valve stem
   into the guide and moving back and forth as shown in the figure.
3. Measure the valve stem oil clearance.
   1. Measure the inside diameter of the valve guide at several places with an inside dial gauge.
   2. Measure the valve stem diameter.
   3. Calculate the clearance between the valve stem and valve guide by subtracting the
      difference where the clearance is the largest.

      | Clearance limit | Value |
      | --------------- | ----- |
      | Intake | 0.08 mm (0.003 in) |
      | Exhaust | 0.10 mm (0.004 in) |

      If the clearance exceeds the limit, replace both valve and guide.

4. Valve guide replacement.
   1. Break the valve guide at combustion chamber.
   2. Heat cylinder head about 100°C (212°F).
   3. From the combustion chamber, drive out the guide toward the top of cylinder head with SST.
      **SST [09201-60011]**

---
<a id="p121"></a>
**[PDF p.121]** — CYLINDER HEAD — Valve, Guide and Seat (cont.) · printed page 5-12

*(Continuation of valve guide replacement.)*

3. With SST, drive in the new guide until the snap ring contacts the cylinder head.
   **SST [09201-60011]**
   > **Note:**
   > 1. Ensure that the hole is clean.
   > 2. Before inserting the guide apply a thin coat of oil to it and the guide hole.

4. Ream the guide to the specified clearance with an 8.5 mm (0.33 in) reamer.

   | Oil clearance standard | Value |
   | ---------------------- | ----- |
   | Intake | 0.025 – 0.055 mm (0.0010 – 0.0022 in) |
   | Exhaust | 0.03 – 0.06 mm (0.0012 – 0.0024 in) |

5. Grinding valves and seats.
   1. Grind all valves to remove the pits and carbon. **Valve face angle: 45.5°**

6. Check the valve head margin and replace if less than specified.

   | Margin limit | Value |
   | ------------ | ----- |
   | Intake | 0.5 mm (0.020 in) |
   | Exhaust | 0.6 mm (0.024 in) |

---
<a id="p122"></a>
**[PDF p.122]** — CYLINDER HEAD — Valve, Guide and Seat (cont.) · printed page 5-13

*(Figs. 5-32 through 5-35.)*

7. Resurface valve seats with 45° carbide cutter. Remove only enough metal to clean seat.
8. Coat valve face with prussian blue or white lead. Locate contact point on valve by
   rotating valve against seat.
   > **Note:** Seat contact should be in middle of valve face with following width:
   >
   > | | Width |
   > | --- | ----- |
   > | Intake | 1.2 – 1.6 mm (0.047 – 0.063 in) |
   > | Exhaust | 1.2 – 1.6 mm (0.047 – 0.063 in) |

9. Correct seat position. To correct seating that is too high, use 30° and 45° cutters. If
   seating is too low, use 60° and 45° cutters.
10. Check valve concentricity. Lightly coat seat with prussian blue. Install valve and rotate.
    If blue appears 360° around face, valve stem and face are concentric. If not, replace valve.

---
<a id="p123"></a>
**[PDF p.123]** — CYLINDER HEAD — Valve Springs · printed page 5-14

11. Check seat/guide concentricity. Apply a light coat of prussian blue to valve face. Install
    and rotate valve. If blue appears 360° around valve seat, guide and seat are concentric. If
    not, recut seat.

### Valve Springs

1. Check the squareness of the valve spring with a steel square and surface plate. Turn the
   spring around slowly and observe the space between the top of the spring and the square.
   Replace the spring if it is out of square more than the specified limit.

   | Item | Value |
   | ---- | ----- |
   | Squareness limit (intake, exhaust) | 1.6 mm (0.063 in) |

2. Measure the spring free height. Replace springs that do not meet specification.

   | Free height | Value |
   | ----------- | ----- |
   | Inner | 45.9 mm (1.807 in) |
   | Outer | 47.4 mm (1.866 in) |

3. Using a spring tester, measure the tension of each spring at the specified installed height.
   Replace any spring that does not meet specification.

   | Installed length | Value |
   | ---------------- | ----- |
   | Inner | 36.5 mm (1.347 in) |
   | Outer | 39.0 mm (1.535 in) |

   | Installed tension | Inner | Outer |
   | ----------------- | ----- | ----- |
   | STD | 7.3 kg (16.1 lb) | 23.7 kg (52.3 lb) |
   | Limit | 6.7 kg (14.8 lb) | 21.8 kg (48.1 lb) |

---
<a id="p124"></a>
**[PDF p.124]** — CYLINDER HEAD — Valve Lifter, Camshaft · printed page 5-15

### Valve Lifter

1. Measure valve lifter oil clearance.
   1. Measure outside diameter of lifter.
   2. Measure inside diameter of cylinder head.

      | Oil clearance | Value |
      | ------------- | ----- |
      | Limit | 0.1 mm (0.004 in) |
      | Standard | 0.02 – 0.03 mm (0.0008 – 0.0012 in) <!-- RESOLVED: metric authoritative (0.02–0.03 mm); imperial recomputed 0.02 mm = 0.0008 in, 0.03 mm = 0.0012 in (printed "0.008" was a manual typo). --> |

2. Valve lifter selective fits.

   | Fit Code (Paint) | Cylinder Head Valve Sleeve Bore | Valve Lifter Outside Diameter |
   | ---------------- | ------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
   | Black | 37.951 – 37.957 mm (1.4941 – 1.4944 in) | 37.925 – 37.931 mm (1.4931 – 1.4933 in) |
   | Blue | 37.957 – 37.963 mm (1.4944 – 1.4946 in) | 37.931 – 37.937 mm (1.4933 – 1.4936 in) |
   | Yellow | 37.963 – 37.969 mm (1.4946 – 1.4948 in) | 37.937 – 37.943 mm (1.4936 – 1.4938 in) |
   | Red | 37.969 – 37.975 mm (1.4948 – 1.4951 in) | 37.943 – 37.949 mm (1.4938 – 1.4941 in) |

### Camshaft and Bearing

1. Check the camshaft for runout. Replace camshaft if it exceeds limit.

   | Item | Value |
   | ---- | ----- |
   | Runout limit | 0.03 mm (0.0012 in) |

---
<a id="p125"></a>
**[PDF p.125]** — CYLINDER HEAD — Camshaft and Bearing (cont.) · printed page 5-16

2. Measure the cam lobe height and check for wear. If wear exceeds the limit, replace the
   camshaft.

   | Item | Value |
   | ---- | ----- |
   | Height limit (intake, exhaust) | 45.0 mm (1.77 in) |

3. Check bearing for flaking or scoring.
4. Measure camshaft oil clearance.
   1. Clean bearing and camshaft.
   2. Place a piece of plastigage across the full width of the journal surface.

---
<a id="p126"></a>
**[PDF p.126]** — CYLINDER HEAD — Camshaft, Manifolds · printed page 5-17

3. Install the bearing cap and tighten bolts to specified torque.

   | Item | Value |
   | ---- | ----- |
   | Torque | 1.2 – 1.8 kg-m (8.7 – 13.0 ft·lb) |

   > **Note:** Do not turn camshaft while plastigage is in place.

4. Remove the bearing cap.
5. With the plastigage scale, measure the width of the plastigage at its widest point. If
   clearance exceeds the specification limit, adjust with a suitable bearing size.

   | Oil clearance | Value |
   | ------------- | ----- |
   | Limit | 0.15 mm (0.0059 in) |
   | Standard | 0.03 – 0.07 mm (0.0012 – 0.0028 in) |

### Manifolds

1. Inspect the cylinder head contacting surfaces for warpage and replace the manifold if it
   exceeds the limit. **Warpage limit 0.1 mm (0.0039 in)** (intake manifold).
2. Inspect the cylinder head contacting surfaces for warpage and replace the manifold if it
   exceeds the limit. **Warpage limit 0.1 mm (0.0039 in)** (exhaust manifold).

### Adjust The Valve Clearance

1. Measure the intake side valve clearance.
   1. Exhaust side valve lifter No.2 and No.4 should protrude the same amount (approx. 2 mm).

   | Intake valve clearance | Value |
   | ---------------------- | ----- |

7. Select a new pad that will give the specified valve clearance as follows.
   1. Measure the pad that was off with a micrometer.
   2. Calculate thickness of new pad so valve clearance comes within specified value.

      - **T₁** ...... Thickness of pad used
      - **A** ......... Valve clearance measured

      **Intake Side** — New Pad Thickness = T₁ + (A − 0.29 mm)

      **Exhaust Side** — New Pad Thickness = T₁ + (A − 0.34 mm)

   3. Select a pad with a thickness as close as possible to the value calculated.
      **Pads are available in 41 sizes, in increments of 0.05 mm (0.002 in), from 1.00 mm
      (0.039 in) to 3.00 mm (0.118 in).**

8. Install parts and valve lifter.

## Timing Chain — Inspection & Repair

### Timing Gear and Chain

1. Inspect the gears and chains for cracks, wear or chipped teeth.
2. Measure the gear for wear in the method shown in the figure. If measurement is below limit,
   replace gears and chain.

   | Wear limit | Value |
   | ---------- | ----- |
   | A: Crankshaft gear | 60.0 mm (2.362 in) |
   | B: Pump drive shaft gear | 114.5 mm (4.503 in) |
   | C: Camshaft drive gear | 78.2 mm (3.079 in) |
   | D: Camshaft timing gear | 78.2 mm (3.079 in) |

3. Measure the elongation of the No.1 timing chain.

   | Elongation limit | Value |
   | ---------------- | ----- |
   | | 291.4 mm (11.47 in) — tension at 5 kg (11 lb) |

4. Measure the 17-link elongation of the No.2 timing chain. Replace the chain if over the
   elongation limit.

   | Elongation limit (at 17-links) | Value |
   | ------------------------------ | ----- |
   | | 147 mm (5.79 in) |

### Timing Gear and Thrust Plate

Measure thrust clearance. If it exceeds limit, replace thrust plate.

1. Inspect distributor drive gear. If damaged, replace, and also inspect distributor gear.
2. Measure oil clearance.
   1. Measure pump drive shaft journal.

      | Finished size | Value |
      | ------------- | ----- |
      | Front | 45.96 – 45.98 mm (1.8094 – 1.8102 in) |
      | Rear | 40.96 – 40.98 mm (1.6126 – 1.6134 in) |

   2. Measure inner diameter of bearing.

      | Oil clearance | Value |
      | ------------- | ----- |
      | Limit | 0.08 mm (0.0032 in) |
      | Standard | 0.03 – 0.07 mm (0.0012 – 0.0028 in) |
