*Section code: `7` | PDF pages 163-175 of `source.pdf` | 13 pages | printed section pages 7-1 … 7-13*

> Faithful transcription. Covers the cooling circuit, the **water pump sectional view**, and
> two water-pump variants that are serviced differently: **without tempered cooling fan** and
> **with tempered (fluid-coupling / bimetal) cooling fan** — each has its own disassembly and
> assembly sequence, kept distinct below. Also covers **radiator** inspection/repair and
> **thermostat** inspection. All press-fit temperatures, clearances, the fluid-coupling
> capacity, the radiator-cap pressures, and the thermostat opening spec were **image-verified**
> against the page images. Two SSTs appear — most steps use **09236-00100**; the pulley-seat
> press-fit on [PDF p.174] uses **09238-40010**. See [llm-instructions.md](llm-instructions.md).

### Water Pump (with tempered cooling fan) — Inspection

1. Inspect the disassembled parts for cracks, damage, and wear, and replace any part found defective.
2. Inspect the bearing. If damaged, produces noise, or does not turn properly, replace the bearing.

### Radiator — Inspection & Repair

1. **Fig. 7-30** — Inspect the radiator core fins, and repair any fins blocking air passage by
   the method as shown.
2. **Fig. 7-31** — Inspect the radiator cap regulation pressure and vacuum valves for spring
   tension and seating. If the pressure gauge drops rapidly and excessively, replace the
   radiator cap.

   | Radiator cap valve opening pressure | Value |
   | --- | --- |
   | Limit | 0.6 kg/cm² (8.5 psi) |
   | Standard | 0.9 kg/cm² (12.8 psi) |

3. **Fig. 7-32** — Inspect the cooling system for leaks. Attach the pressure tester to the
   radiator and pump it to the specified pressure. If the pressure gauge drops, inspect all
   hoses and fittings for an external leak. If no external leak is found, an internal intake
   manifold, block, or heater core leak should be suspected.

### Thermostat — Inspection

1. Replace if the valve remains open at normal temperature or does not have proper tightness
   when fully closed.
2. **Fig. 7-33** — Immerse the thermostat in water, and check the valve opening temperatures
   by heating the water gradually. The valve is satisfactory if it starts to open at
   **88°C (190°C)** and opens to more than **8 mm (0.32 in)** at **100°C (212°F)**. Replace if
   necessary.
