### Starting System Circuit

Starter wiring diagram, shown both pictorially (Fig. 9-1) and schematically (Fig. 9-2). The
circuit runs from the battery through the **fusible link** and **ignition switch** (via the
**neutral start switch** on A/T vehicles) to the magnetic switch's **pull-in** and **hold-in
coils**, and through the **field coils**, **armature** and **brushes**. Key terminals: **"30"
terminal**, **"50" terminal**, and **"C" terminal**. *(Diagrams — see the source page image.)*

## INSPECTION AND REPAIR

### Armature Shaft & Bearings

1. Inspect armature shaft end, drive housing bushing and end frame bushing for wear or
   damage. *(Fig. 9-11)*

   | Item | Limit |
   | --- | --- |
   | Oil clearance limit | 0.2 mm (0.008 in) |

2. Bushing replacement.
   1. Pry out the bushing cover and press out the bushing. *(Fig. 9-12)*
   2. Aligning the bushing hole with the housing groove, press in new bushing.
   3. Ream bushing to obtain the specified clearance. *(Fig. 9-13)*

      | Item | Standard |
      | --- | --- |
      | Oil clearance | 0.10 – 0.14 mm (0.0039 – 0.0055 in) |

   4. Clean the bore, and install new bushing cover. *(Fig. 9-14)*

### Commutator

Inspect for the following items and repair or replace.

1. Dirty or burnt surface. Correct by sandpaper or lathe if necessary. *(Figs. 9-15, 9-16)*
   Use #400 sandpaper.
2. Runout: Correct on a lathe if it exceeds the limit. *(Fig. 9-17)*

   | Item | Standard | Limit |
   | --- | --- | --- |
   | Runout | 0.05 mm (0.0020 in) | 0.4 mm (0.016 in) |

3. Surface wear: If below the limit, replace armature. *(Fig. 9-18)*

   | Item | Standard | Limit |
   | --- | --- | --- |
   | Commutator diameter | 32.7 mm (1.287 in) | 31 mm (1.22 in) |

### Armature Coil

1. Ground test. Check commutator and armature coil core. If there is continuity, the armature
   is grounded and must be replaced. *(Fig. 9-23)*
2. Open-circuit test. Check for continuity between the segments. If there is no continuity at
   any test point, there is an open-circuit and armature must be replaced. *(Fig. 9-24)*

### Field Coil

1. Open circuit test. Check for continuity between the lead wire and field coil brush soldered
   connection. If there is no continuity, there is an open-circuit in the field coil, and it
   should be replaced. *(Fig. 9-25)*
2. Ground test. Check for continuity between field coil end and field frame. If there is
   continuity, repair or replace the field coil. *(Fig. 9-26)*

### Starter Clutch and Pinion Gear

1. Inspect spline teeth for wear and damage. Replace if necessary. *(Fig. 9-31)*
2. Inspect pinion for smooth movement.
3. Inspect pinion gear teeth and chamfer if worn or damaged. *(Fig. 9-32)*
4. Rotate pinion. It should turn free in clockwise direction and lock when turned
   counterclockwise. *(Fig. 9-33)*

### Magnetic Switch

1. Push in plunger and release it. The plunger should return quickly to its original position.
   *(Fig. 9-34)*

### Magnetic Switch (cont.)

2. Measure distance from switch mounting surface to stud end. *(Fig. 9-35)*

   | Item | Standard |
   | --- | --- |
   | Mounting-surface-to-stud-end distance | approx. 34 mm (1.34 in) |

   To adjust, loosen the lock nut and screw stud in or out.
3. Pull-in coil open circuit test. Check for continuity between the "50" terminal and "C"
   terminal. *(Fig. 9-36)*
4. Hold-in coil open-circuit test. Check for continuity between the "50" terminal and switch
   body. *(Fig. 9-37)*
5. I.G. terminal continuity test. Push in plunger until it stops. Check for continuity between
   "30" terminal and lead wire. *(Fig. 9-38)*

> **Note:** Perform the switch operation test after assembling it to the motor.

### Assembly (cont.) — Thrust clearance & grease

- Check that the (+) wires are not grounded: *(Fig. 9-48)*
  - Field coil
  - Brush (+) leads
  - Through bolts
- Install the lock plate and measure the armature shaft thrust clearance. If clearance exceeds
  the specified value, correct by increasing the number of shims. *(Fig. 9-49; the extra
  snap-ring/spring shown is only for 1.0 kW)*

  | Item | Value |
  | --- | --- |
  | Thrust clearance | 0.05–0.35 mm (0.002–0.0138 in) |
  | Adjusting shim thickness | 0.5 mm (0.02 in) |

- Install end frame cap not more than half full of grease. *(Fig. 9-50)*
- Hook the magnetic switch joint on the drive lever spring from the lower side. *(Fig. 9-51)*

## PERFORMANCE TEST

Check the magnetic switch performance and pinion gap as follows.

> **Precaution:** These tests must be performed in short time (3–5 seconds) to prevent the coil
> from burning. Disconnect the field coil lead from "C" terminal.

1. **Pull-in test.** Connect magnetic switch to battery as shown (negative side to "C" terminal
   and switch body; positive side to "50" terminal). If the pinion has definitely jumped out,
   the pull-in coil is satisfactory. *(Fig. 9-52)*
2. **Hold-in test.** Disconnect the "C" terminal. The pinion should remain projected. *(Fig.
   9-53)*
3. **Check the plunger return.** When disconnecting the switch body, the pinion should return
   quickly. *(Fig. 9-54)*

---

### Performance Test (cont.) — Pinion clearance & no-load

4. **Check the pinion clearance.** *(Figs. 9-55, 9-56, 9-57)*
   1. Connect the magnetic switch to battery as shown:
      - Field coil lead to "C" terminal
      - Battery negative side to body
      - Battery positive side to "50" terminal
   2. Move the pinion to armature side to eliminate the slack, and check the clearance between
      the pinion end and stop collar.

      | Item | Standard clearance |
      | --- | --- |
      | Pinion clearance | 1.0–4.0 mm (0.04–0.16 in) |

   3. Adjust if necessary after loosening lock nut:

      | Clearance | Stud |
      | --- | --- |
      | Too large | Screw in |
      | Too small | Screw out |

5. **No-load performance test.** Connect the field coil lead to the "C" terminal, making sure
   that the lead wire is not grounded. Connect starter to battery. If the starter shows smooth
   and steady rotation with the pinion jumping out and draws less than the specified current, it
   is satisfactory. *(Fig. 9-58)*

   | Item | Value |
   | --- | --- |
   | Specified current | Less than 50 A |
