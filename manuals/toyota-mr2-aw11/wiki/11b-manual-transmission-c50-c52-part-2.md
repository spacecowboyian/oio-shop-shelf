## Input Shaft Assembly

### Disassembly of Input Shaft Assembly

**1. Measure Third and Fourth Gear Thrust Clearance**

Using a feeler gauge, measure the thrust clearance.

| Gear | Standard clearance | Maximum clearance |
|---|---|---|
| 3rd gear | 0.10 – 0.35 mm (0.0039 – 0.0138 in.) | 0.40 mm (0.0157 in.) |
| 4th gear | 0.10 – 0.55 mm (0.0039 – 0.0217 in.) | 0.60 mm (0.0236 in.) |

**2. Check Oil Clearance of Third and Fourth Gear**

Using a dial indicator, measure the oil clearance between the gear and shaft.

| Gear | Standard clearance | Maximum clearance |
|---|---|---|
| 3rd gear | 0.009 – 0.053 mm (0.0004 – 0.0020 in.) | 0.070 mm (0.0028 in.) |
| 4th gear | 0.009 – 0.051 mm (0.0004 – 0.0020 in.) | 0.070 mm (0.0028 in.) |

If the clearance exceeds the limit, replace the gear, needle roller bearing or shaft.

**3. Remove Snap Ring**

Using two screwdrivers and a hammer, tap out the snap ring.

**4. Remove Radial Ball Bearing and Fourth Gear**

Using SST and a press, remove the radial ball bearing.
- SST 09950-00020

**5. Remove Needle Roller Bearings, Spacer and Synchronizer Ring**

**6. Remove Snap Ring**

Using two screwdrivers and a hammer, tap out the snap ring.

**7. Remove No. 2 Clutch Hub Assembly, Synchronizer Ring and Third Gear**

Using a press, remove the No. 2 hub sleeve, 3rd gear, synchronizer ring and needle roller bearings.

**8. Remove Needle Roller Bearing**

**9. Remove Input Shaft Front Bearing Inner Race**

Using SST and a press, remove the inner race.
- SST 09950-00020

<a id="p473"></a>
### Inspection of Input Shaft Component Parts

**1. Inspect Synchronizer Rings**

1. Check for wear or damage.
2. Turn the ring and push it in to check the braking action.
3. Measure the clearance between the synchronizer ring back and the gear spline end.
   - **Minimum clearance:** 0.6 mm (0.024 in.)
   - If the clearance is less than the limit, replace the synchronizer ring.

**2. Measure Clearance of No. 2 Shift Fork and Hub Sleeve**

Using a feeler gauge, measure the clearance between the hub sleeve and shift fork.
- **Maximum clearance:** 1.0 mm (0.039 in.)
- If the clearance exceeds the limit, replace the shift fork or hub sleeve.

**3. Inspect Input Shaft**

1. Check the input shaft for wear or damage.
2. Using a micrometer, measure the outer diameter of the input shaft journal surface.

   | Journal | Minimum outer diameter |
   |---|---|
   | Part A | 32.930 mm (1.2965 in.) |
   | Part B and C | 35.950 mm (1.4154 in.) |

3. Using a dial indicator, check the shaft runout.
   - **Maximum runout:** 0.05 mm (0.0020 in.)

<a id="p474"></a>

1. Install the spacer.
2. Apply MP grease to the needle roller bearings.

## Oil Pump Assembly

*(Figure: exploded view of the oil pump assembly — see page image p.482 [MT-65]. `+` = non-reusable part.)*

**Oil pump cover bolt torque (from exploded view):** 105 kg-cm (8 ft-lb, 10 N·m)

<a id="p482"></a>
### Disassembly of Oil Pump

**1. Check Operation of Oil Pump**

Install the oil pump drive gear to the drive rotor, and check that the drive rotor turns smoothly.

**2. Remove Gasket to Oil Pump Case**

**3. Remove Bolt and Oil Strainer**

**4. Remove Oil Pump Cover**

1. Hold the oil pump cover, remove the two bolts and cover.
2. Remove the spring holder, spring, ball and relief valve seat.

**5. Check Rotor Body Clearance**

1. Install the oil pump drive gear to the drive rotor.
2. Using a feeler gauge, measure the body clearance between the drive rotor and oil pump case.
   - **Standard clearance:** 0.10 – 0.16 mm (0.004 – 0.006 in.)
   - **Maximum clearance:** 0.30 mm (0.012 in.)

**6. Check Rotor Tip Clearance**

1. Install the oil pump drive gear to the drive rotor.
2. Using a feeler gauge, measure the tip clearance between the drive and driven rotors.
   - **Standard clearance:** 0.08 – 0.15 mm (0.003 – 0.006 in.)
   - **Maximum clearance:** 0.30 mm (0.012 in.)

**7. Check Side Clearance**

Using a precision straight edge and feeler gauge, measure the side clearance of both rotors.
- **Standard clearance:** 0.03 – 0.08 mm (0.001 – 0.003 in.)
- **Maximum clearance:** 0.15 mm (0.006 in.)

**8. Remove Oil Pump Drive Rotor and Driven Rotor**

**9. If Necessary, Replace O-Ring**

1. Using a screwdriver, remove the O-ring.
2. Apply gear oil to the O-ring.
3. Install the O-ring.

<a id="p485"></a>
### Assembly of Oil Pump

**1. (Only when replacing oil pump case) Install Tight Plug**

1. Using a pin punch and hammer, drive in a new tight plug.
   - **Drive-in depth:** 0.20 – 0.90 mm (0.008 – 0.035 in.)
2. Using a screwdriver and hammer, stake the three points.

**2. Install Driven Rotor and Drive Rotor**

**3. Install Oil Pump Cover**

1. Install the relief valve, ball, spring and spring holder to the oil pump case.
2. Hold the oil pump cover, and temporarily install the two bolts.

**4. Install Oil Strainer**

Install the oil strainer to the oil pump case, and temporarily install the bolt.

**5. Torque Oil Pump Cover Bolts**

Torque the three bolts evenly.
- **Torque:** 105 kg-cm (8 ft-lb, 10 N·m)

**6. Check Operation of Oil Pump**

Insert the oil pump drive gear to the drive rotor, and check that the drive rotor turns smoothly.

**7. Install Gasket**

Install the new gasket to the oil pump case.

## Differential

*(Figure: exploded view of the differential — see page image p.491 [MT-74]. `+` = non-reusable part, `*` = precoated part.)*

**Transaxle case cover bolt/nut torque (from exploded view):** 550 kg-cm (40 ft-lb, 54 N·m)

<a id="p492"></a>
### Disassembly of Differential Case

**1. Remove Side Bearing**

Using SST, remove the two side bearings.
- SST 09950-20017

**2. Remove Ring Gear**

1. Place matchmarks on both the differential case and ring gear.
2. Remove the sixteen bolts.
3. Using a plastic hammer, tap out the ring gear.

**3. Disassemble Differential Case**

1. Place matchmarks on the differential right and left case.
2. Using a torx wrench, remove the sixteen torx screws.
   - Torx wrench T50 09042-00040
3. Using a plastic hammer, tap out the differential left case.
4. Remove the speedometer drive gear from the differential right case.
5. Remove the two differential side gears, two side thrust washers, differential spider, four differential pinions and four pinion washers from the differential left case.

**4. (Transmission Case Side) If Necessary, Replace Oil Seal and Taper Roller Bearing Outer Race**

1. Using a screwdriver, remove the oil seal.
2. Remove the transmission oil baffle.
3. Using a brass bar and hammer, drive out the bearing outer race lightly and evenly.
4. Remove the shim.
5. Install the shim. (See exploded view, page image p.491 [MT-74].)
   > **NOTE:** First select and install a shim of lesser thickness than before.
6. Using SST and a press, install the taper roller bearing outer race.
   - SST 09316-60010 (09316-00010, 09316-00040)
7. Install the transmission oil baffle.
   > **NOTE:** Install the transmission oil baffle projection into the case side cutout.
8. Using SST and a hammer, drive in a new oil seal.
   - SST 09223-15010
9. Coat the lip of the oil seal with MP grease.

**5. (Transaxle Case Side) If Necessary, Replace Oil Seal and Taper Roller Bearing Outer Race**

1. Remove the four bolts and three nuts.
2. Using a plastic hammer, tap the stud bolt and remove the transaxle case cover.
3. Using a screwdriver and hammer, drive out the oil seal.
4. Using SST, a brass bar and hammer, remove the taper roller bearing outer race.
   - SST 09612-65014
5. Using SST and a hammer, install the taper roller bearing.
   - SST 09316-60010 (09316-00010, 09316-00040)
6. Using SST and a hammer, drive in a new oil seal.
   - SST 09316-60010 (09316-00010)
7. Coat the lip of the oil seal with MP grease.
8. Remove any packing material and be careful not to drop oil on the contacting surfaces of the transaxle case or case cover.
9. Apply seal packing to the transaxle case cover as shown in the figure.
   - **Seal packing:** Part No. 08826-00090, THREE BOND 1281 or equivalent
   > **NOTE:** Install the transaxle case cover as soon as the seal packing is applied.
10. Apply liquid sealant to the bolt threads.
    - **Sealant:** Part No. 08833-00080, THREE BOND 1344, LOCTITE 242 or equivalent
11. Install and torque the four bolts and three nuts.
    - **Torque:** 550 kg-cm (40 ft-lb, 54 N·m)

**6. Assemble Differential Case**

> **NOTE:** Coat all of the sliding and rotating surfaces with gear oil before assembly.

1. Install the thrust washer to the side gear.
2. Install the four pinions and thrust washers to the spider.
3. Install the side gear and spider with four pinions to the differential left case.
4. Using a dial indicator, measure the backlash of one pinion gear while holding the No. 2 differential case.
   - **Standard backlash:** 0.05 – 0.20 mm (0.0020 – 0.0079 in.)
   > **NOTE:** Push the pinion gear on the left side of the differential case.
5. Install the side gear and spider with four pinions to the right side of the differential case, and check the side gear backlash.
6. Referring to the table below, select the thrust washer which will ensure that the backlash is within specification. Try to select a washer of the same size.

   | Mark | Thickness mm (in.) | Mark | Thickness mm (in.) |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | — | 0.80 (0.0315) | — | 1.20 (0.0472) |
   | — | 0.90 (0.0354) | — | 1.30 (0.0512) |
   | — | 1.00 (0.0394) | — | 1.40 (0.0551) |
   | — | 1.10 (0.0433) | | |

7. Install the speedometer driven gear.
8. Align the matchmarks on the differential cases.
9. Using a plastic hammer, carefully tap the differential case.
10. Using a torx wrench, install and torque the sixteen torx screws.
    - Torx wrench T50 09042-00040
    - **Torque:** 640 kg-cm (46 ft-lb, 63 N·m)

**7. Install Ring Gear**

1. Clean the contact surface of the differential case.
2. Heat the ring gear to about 100°C (212°F) in an oil bath.
   > **CAUTION:** Do not heat the ring gear above 110°C (230°F).
3. Clean the contact surface of the ring gear with cleaning solvent.
4. Quickly install the ring gear on the differential case. Install the sixteen bolts.
   > **NOTE:** Align the matchmarks on the differential left case and contact the ring gear.
5. Tighten the set bolts uniformly and a little at a time. Torque the bolts.
   - **Torque:** 1,260 kg-cm (91 ft-lb, 124 N·m)

**8. Install Side Bearing**

Using SST and a press, install the side bearings onto the differential case.
- SST 09316-60010 (09316-00010)
> **NOTE:** Press the bearing on the ring gear side first.

**9. Adjust Output Shaft Assembly Preload**

(See steps under [Installation of Component Parts](#installation-of-component-parts), MT-84 to MT-85.)

**10. Install Differential Case Assembly**

Install the differential case assembly to the transaxle case.

**11. Install Output Shaft Assembly**

Lift up the differential case, and install the input shaft assembly.

**12. Install Transmission Case**

1. Install the transmission case.
   > **NOTE:** If necessary, tap on the case with a plastic hammer.
2. Install and torque the seventeen bolts.
   - **Torque:** 300 kg-cm (22 ft-lb, 29 N·m)

**13. Install Output Shaft Rear Taper Roller Bearing Outer Race**

**14. Install Shim**

(See steps under [Installation of Component Parts](#installation-of-component-parts), MT-84 to MT-85.)
> **NOTE:** Install the previously selected shim.

**15. Install Rear Bearing Retainer**

Using a torx wrench, install and torque the seven torx screws.
- Torx wrench T45 09042-00050
- **Torque:** 430 kg-cm (31 ft-lb, 42 N·m)

2. Turn the output shaft right and left two or three times to allow the bearings to settle.
3. Using a torque wrench, measure the preload.

   If the preload is not within specification, select the thrust washers.
   > **NOTE:** The total preload will change about 1 – 2 kg-cm (0.9 – 1.7 in.-lb, 0.1 – 0.2 N·m) with each shim thickness.

   | Mark | Thickness mm (in.) | Mark | Thickness mm (in.) |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | 0 | 2.00 (0.0787) | 9 | 2.45 (0.0965) |
   | 1 | 2.05 (0.0807) | A | 2.50 (0.0984) |
   | 2 | 2.10 (0.0827) | B | 2.55 (0.1004) |
   | 3 | 2.15 (0.0846) | C | 2.60 (0.1024) |
   | 4 | 2.20 (0.0866) | D | 2.65 (0.1043) |
   | 5 | 2.25 (0.0886) | E | 2.70 (0.1063) |
   | 6 | 2.30 (0.0906) | F | 2.75 (0.1083) |
   | 7 | 2.35 (0.0925) | G | 2.80 (0.1102) |
   | 8 | 2.40 (0.0945) | H | 2.85 (0.1122) |

**17. Remove Rear Bearing Retainer**

Using a torx wrench, remove the seven torx screws and rear bearing retainer.
- Torx wrench T45 09042-00050

**18. Remove Shim**

**19. Remove Transmission Case**

Remove the seventeen bolts and tap off the case with a plastic hammer.

**2. Install Oil Pump Assembly and Oil Pipe**
